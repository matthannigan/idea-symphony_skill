# Persona Selection Guide (Phase 2B)

This document provides the orchestrator with structured guidance for selecting question-generation personas and setting per-persona volume targets in Phase 2B.

---

## Tier 1 Question Personas (10)

Phase 2B draws from a pool of 10 personas validated for question generation. These personas are at the core of all Idea Symphony question generation. While other personas are also sometimes additive, most Idea Symphony brainstorms will be driven by questions created by these personas.

### Per-Persona Guidance

| Persona | Guidance | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Questioner** | Include in nearly all rosters as the meta-question generator to widen problem framing before more specialized personas fire. Pair with Devil's Advocate and a human-centered persona (Storyteller or Audience Advocate) to turn analytical probes into lived-experience scenarios. | Dimensional profile ~49% analytical, 39% human-centered, 28% provocative. Native question persona; high volume, multi-dimensional. Very high selflessness — many questions explicitly open territory for other personas. Low convergence (~13%) with First Principles Thinker despite both being analytical. Filler resistance is exceptionally strong (~2-3% at 15-20, onset ~22+). Significant volume overshoot tendency on complex/multi-stakeholder topics (40% overshoot rate) — post-hoc count validation recommended. |
| **Analyst** | Include whenever analytical coverage is desired, especially alongside Devil's Advocate. Combine with a generative persona (Visionary or Analogist) to approximate full CPS Clarify-Ideate-Develop coverage. | Dimensional profile ~71% analytical with strategic and mild systems coverage; zero provocative questions. Highly selfless — any Phase 3 persona can answer. Clean 5-cluster outputs with low variance (CV~9.7%). Complementary with (not redundant to) Devil's Advocate — no direct cluster overlap. Risk of analytical bias arises only if paired exclusively with other analytical/evaluative personas with no generative counterweights. |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Use in all but the most lighthearted sessions. Pair with Appreciative Inquirer or Visionary to maintain balance between risk discovery and aspirational exploration, countering framing bias. | Dimensional profile ~53% provocative with strong human-centered and tactical sub-dimensions; only ~2% analytical. Topic-adaptive — volume expands on complex topics with many "attack surfaces." Functions as Six Hats Black Hat; must be deliberately balanced by generative personas to avoid pessimistic anchoring. |
| **Audience Advocate** | Include when user explicitly cares about marginalized stakeholders, accessibility, or real-world community impacts. Pair with Analyst (for metrics and frameworks) and Storyteller (for concrete user journeys) to turn structural concerns into rich question sets. | Dimensional profile ~60% human-centered, ~20% systems. Structurally-aware human-centeredness unique among human-centered personas — questions about documentation barriers, equity frameworks, and systemic inclusion open territory for analytical, systems, and practical personas. Higher selflessness than Empath (60% vs 33%). Low overlap with Storyteller (~12%) and Appreciative Inquirer (~11%). Cross-volume stable — equity lens recognizable even at low volumes. |
| **Systems Thinker** | Include when the topic is infrastructural, policy, or ecosystem-level. Pair with Visionary/Analogist and Devil's Advocate for a full range of system futures and risks. Without generative partners, can skew toward diagnostic rather than imaginative questioning. | Dimensional profile ~50% systems, ~21% human-centered. Named system archetypes (Fixes that Fail, Shifting the Burden, etc.) are unique vocabulary across all personas. Fills the single most underrepresented dimension in the roster. Exceptional consistency (CV=2.2%). ~56-60% unique territory vs Tier 1 aggregate. HIGH selflessness (~60-65%). |
| **Appreciative Inquirer** | Include whenever there is risk that analytical or risk personas will dominate framing. Pair with Devil's Advocate and Analyst to create a dual lens of "what's strong" and "what can break." Cannot function as standalone question generator — deficit-avoidance is complete (zero risk/failure questions). | ~39% strict strengths-based questions (3-6x the rate of any other persona). Dimension profile nearly inverse of the analytical set — strong Creative (26%) and Strategic (18%) where the set is weak. ~70% selflessness. Very low topic sensitivity — 4-D structure stable across all topic types. ~18% convergence with Visionary, ~22% with Empath, ~12% with Audience Advocate. |
| **Provocateur** | Use when the risk of fixation is high or when the user explicitly wants non-obvious, edgy perspectives. Always pair with at least one strong analytical persona and one human-centered persona to ensure provocations are grounded and ethically framed. | Dimensional profile ~52% provocative with creative as frequent secondary. Cluster structures uniquely subversive (e.g., "The Anti-Library"). Extremely powerful lateral disruptor. Cross-topic consistency stronger than expected — concrete-topic weakness does not manifest at the 5-8 range (was a volume effect, not a topic effect). Natural set point of ~7 questions with universal 3-cluster structure. |
| **Analogist** | Use alongside Visionary and Provocateur to provide multiple distinct paths to divergence: reimagination (Visionary), provocation (Provocateur), and analogy (Analogist). For highly technical or regulated domains, pair with a grounding persona to ensure imported analogies respect real constraints. | Distinctive cluster structures organized by source domain (biological, historical, industry). Introduces conceptual frameworks (mycorrhizal networks, desire lines, keystone habits) no analytical persona generates. Somewhat "selfish" — best positioned to answer its own questions — but imported frameworks create scaffolding other personas can build on. Reliable volume (CV=10.1%). Concrete-topic weakness does not manifest at the 5-8 range — quality is consistently strong across all topic types. Universal 3-cluster structure ideal for round-robin append. Mycorrhizal network analogy recurs across ~40% of topics (each application is distinct). |
| **Visionary** | Include when the task calls for reimagining categories (e.g., new products, systems redesign). Pair with Analyst and Systems Thinker for grounding. Combine with Appreciative Inquirer to connect aspirational futures to existing strengths rather than pure blue-sky. | Operates via reconstructive aspiration, not destructive challenge — complementary to Provocateur. Clean generative persona; high selflessness. Blank-slate reimagination distinct from Appreciative Inquirer's build-from-strengths (~18% convergence). Universal 3-cluster structure; self-contained questions ideal for append. Space-party remains weakest but acceptable — no topic type exposes significant weakness at 5-8. |
| **Storyteller** | Include whenever user experience, engagement, or emotional journey matters, or when countering an overly analytical question set. Pair with Audience Advocate and Empath for rich, multi-perspective human-centered question spaces, and with Systems Thinker to connect micro-stories to macro-patterns. | Most distinctive questions of all Perspective personas — narrative-arc clusters (Arrival, Hero's Journey, etc.) no analytical persona approximates. Highest selflessness among Perspective tier — narrative scenarios invite Empath, Visionary, Pragmatist, Audience Advocate responses. Technical topics handled well via user-experience anchoring rather than narrativizing technology. Natural set point ~7 questions with universal 3-cluster structure. |

### Persona Categories

Personas are organized into three categories based on how their question quality scales with volume.

| Category | Personas | Question Range | Rationale |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Perspective** (narrow lens, distinctive voice) | Provocateur, Analogist, Visionary, Storyteller | **5-8** | Voice becomes formulaic beyond 8; value is in concentrated insight |
| **Structural** (systematic coverage through a framework) | Systems Thinker, Audience Advocate, Devil's Advocate, Appreciative Inquirer | **8-12** | Framework provides natural structure; ~3 questions per cluster |
| **Analytical** (breadth-seeking, systematic coverage) | Questioner, Analyst | **12-15** | Thrives on volume; uses additional questions for genuine new territory |

### Per-Persona Volume Recommendations

| Persona | Category | Recommended Range | Filler Onset | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Questioner** | Analytical | 12-20 | ~22+ Qs | Only persona that genuinely benefits from 15-20. Filler resistance better than predicted (~2-3% at 15-20 vs earlier ~5% estimate). Volume overshoot on complex topics (40% rate); post-hoc count validation recommended. |
| **Analyst** | Analytical | 10-15 | ~15 Qs | Strong systematic coverage. Fragments into narrow sub-questions beyond 15. |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Structural | 8-12 | ~12 Qs | Most impactful focused on structural flaws. Beyond 12, shifts from vulnerabilities to minor operational concerns. |
| **Audience Advocate** | Structural | 10-15 | ~13 Qs | Many distinct stakeholder dimensions justify higher end of Structural range. Lowest filler of any Structural persona. |
| **Systems Thinker** | Structural | 10-15 | ~12 Qs | Enough distinct archetypes and subsystems. Becomes pedantic beyond 15. Quality strongest on complex multi-element topics. |
| **Appreciative Inquirer** | Structural | 8-12 | ~12 Qs | 4-phase AI framework (Discovery, Dream, Design, Destiny) provides natural boundaries. 2-3 questions per phase is optimal. |
| **Provocateur** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~8 Qs | Concentrated burst of assumption-breaking. Beyond 8, provocations become formulaic and shock value diminishes. Highest filler rate (30-40%) at 15-20. |
| **Analogist** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~10 Qs | A few powerful reframing metaphors, not an exhaustive catalogue. Quality drops sharply beyond 10 (surface-level parallels). |
| **Visionary** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~10 Qs | Most powerful when focused on a few genuinely transformative reframings. Often undershoots high targets naturally. |
| **Storyteller** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~8 Qs | Value is in emotional resonance and user journey illumination. Narrative framing becomes forced beyond 8. |

## Tier 2 Question Personas (4)

These personas are included only at **high effort**. They bring specialized approaches that complement Tier 1 perspectives. All four are topic-gated — include based on topic affinity, not by default.

| Persona | Guidance | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Constraint Flipper** | Include as a secondary generative persona for topics where constraints are heavily felt (tight budgets, regulations, physical limitations). | Genuine constraint-inversion operation — asks “how is this limitation actually better?” rather than “how do we work within this?” Produces 14-17 questions consistently (CV=3.7%), notably lower volume than other generative personas. The inversion operation is powerful but exhaustible — once each major constraint is flipped, the persona runs out of moves. Underlying constraints it identifies (budget, time, space) overlap with analytical personas; the *direction* of the question is what’s distinctive. Conceptually close to Lateral Thinking “challenge” technique; particularly helpful against problem-framed prompts that might otherwise induce defensive analysis. Lowest selflessness of the generative tier — questions mostly require its own reframing skill to answer fully. |
| **Empath** | Add when emotional resonance and psychological safety are central concerns, such as wellness, education, or sensitive community work. | Operates inside-out via emotional excavation — “how does it feel?” vs Audience Advocate’s outside-in “how might we design for?” Dimensional profile ~75% human-centered, ~10% creative. Extreme consistency across topics (CV=1.4%). Unique territory includes parental emotional subtext, shame/worthiness dynamics, emotional labor sustainability, and meta-cognitive questions (“What does this app believe about human nature?”). ~25-30% convergence with Storyteller (both care about emotional experience but through different entry points — felt states vs narrative arcs). Lower selflessness than Audience Advocate (33% vs 60%) — most Empath questions channel through human-centered Phase 3 personas. Distinctiveness degrades at lower volumes, becoming harder to distinguish from Storyteller and Appreciative Inquirer. Best in combination with Storyteller and Audience Advocate, where Empath’s gut signals can seed deeper narrative and equity questions. |
| **First Principles Thinker** | Add when high-effort rosters allow more analytical bandwidth or when domain complexity demands deep structural modeling (e.g., data architecture, behavior change mechanics). | Ontological/definitional cognitive mode — “What IS this, fundamentally?” and “What are the necessary and sufficient conditions?” Dimensional profile ~87% analytical with ~27% systems secondary, but behavioral category is **Structural** — organizes around 3 fixed meta-clusters and exhausts distinctive contribution by 10-11 questions, matching Structural natural ceiling. ~43-47% overlap with Questioner (both challenge assumptions, but FPT uses rigorous reductionism vs Questioner’s broader curiosity). ~35-40% unique territory vs Tier 1 aggregate — lower than Systems Thinker’s ~56-60%. Many questions are self-answering, reducing cross-roster selflessness (~45-50%) relative to Questioner. ~60-70% of FPT’s value could be captured by adding first-principles instructions to Questioner; the remaining 30-40% justifies a dedicated persona slot only in high-effort rosters or for domains with deep structural complexity. |
| **Futurist** | Include when topic is civic, infrastructural, or technical; omit for purely personal/experiential contexts. | Cognitive mode is trend extrapolation, not creative reimagination — dimensional profile 56% Strategic, 21% Analytical, 0% Creative. Despite “forward-looking” branding, operates as a strategic persona, not a Visionary alternative. ~30-35% thematic convergence with Visionary but treatment is consistently different (strategic positioning vs paradigm reimagination). ~29% temporal lens anti-pattern rate overall (41% on experiential topics, ~22% on civic/technical) — questions that are standard planning questions with trend decoration bolted on. Moderate selflessness — many questions invite trend-based answers rather than diverse perspectives. Strongest on civic and technical topics where external trends genuinely shape the project. Most structurally consistent persona evaluated (CV=4.4%). |

## Tier 3 Question Personas (5)

These personas are not included by default in any question generations. They are included only when **specifically selected by the orchestrator** based on topic affinity, at medium or high effort. Effort level controls persona count (selection threshold strictness), not volume per persona. At medium effort, only strong triggers justify inclusion (0-1 persona). At high effort, moderate-or-better triggers allow inclusion (0-2 personas). Their questions are handled via synthesis or append depending on the persona.

| Persona | Guidance | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Accountant** | Include only for topics where financial structure is central (business models, funding mechanisms, community budgeting). Topic keywords that increase selection priority: “business,” “nonprofit,” “budget,” “startup,” “pricing,” “revenue.” | Tier 1 financial coverage is 18.4% overall but highly uneven — adequate for community topics (~30%), significant gap for product/business topics (~10.5%). The Analyst carries 61% of Tier 1’s financial questions but stops at framework-level analysis. The Accountant picks up where the Analyst leaves off: quantified benchmarks (dollar ranges, percentages), depreciation/asset lifecycle, reserve fund structuring, insurance categories, unit economics chains with actual numbers, opportunity cost quantification. Complementary with Analyst — Analyst creates analytical structure, Accountant fills it with financial data and modeling. Pair with Appreciative Inquirer or Visionary so economic questions do not overconstrain innovation. |
| **Connector** | Use as an **Analogist replacement** (not alongside) when the topic requires bridging, merging, or reconciling distinct systems that constrain each other. Keep the Analogist when the topic has a single primary domain that benefits from metaphorical reframing. Default: Analogist. | Operates in three sub-modes: cross-domain import (~35%, overlaps with Analogist), structural isomorphism (~30%, unique — “how are X and Y actually the same problem?”), and recombinant innovation (~35%, unique — “what if we combined X + Y?”). Distinctive value depends on non-import mode activation: on inter-domain reconciliation topics (career transitions across fields, institutional mergers, cross-disciplinary design), isomorphism and recombination activate and outperform the Analogist. On single-domain topics (event planning, single-business strategy), falls back to 63-100% import mode indistinguishable from Analogist. Volume 7-8 (mean 7.7), exactly 3 clusters, zero filler — inherits Analogist's append mechanics cleanly. Longer questions (~80-120 words vs Analogist's ~50-80). |
| **Lawyer** | Add only when regulatory/compliance exposure is part of the brainstorming need; trigger on regulatory exposure, not legal domain keywords. Otherwise, let Devil’s Advocate flag high-level risk. | Rigid 4-cluster structure at 8-10 volume (not 7-8 clusters as earlier predicted — that may emerge only at higher volumes). Dense compound questions (80-120 words, 3-4 sub-issues per question) benefit from disaggregation during synthesis. Strongly evaluative and problem-framed — high specificity but heavy negative framing and low generative expansion. Critical for regulated or liability-heavy topics: 80-95% of questions add unique value with specific regulatory mechanisms no Tier 1 persona would identify. Acts like an extreme Black Hat; given anchoring risk, questions should be intentionally mixed with appreciative and generative ones in synthesis to avoid defensive ideation. Career-change is a partial false positive — the persona responds to the keyword "lawyer" and produces a regulatory implementation guide rather than decision-exploration questions. |
| **Politician** | Deploy for civic, institutional, or organizational change topics; unnecessary for personal or purely technical questions. Phase 2C: **Append** (not Synthesis). | Distinctive political-analytical vocabulary — veto players, coalition sequencing, political capital, governance structure, opposition management — that would be stripped by synthesis. Low Tier 1 overlap (~10-15% Audience Advocate, ~15-20% Devil’s Advocate) supports append. Verbose questions (40-80 words at 8-10 volume) deliver content comparable to ~14-15 from more concise personas. Topic sensitivity is nuanced: quality gap manifests as depth ceiling difference (strong-trigger: depth to 12, weak-trigger: depth to 10-11) rather than volume doubling. Produces genuinely useful organizational-political analysis for any topic with stakeholder complexity. |
| **Technical Expert** | Add only for genuine technical architecture: engineering tradeoffs, hardware-software co-design, ML/AI systems. Highest topic sensitivity of any Tier 3 persona — quality gap between strong and weak triggers is the largest observed. | Rigid structure (exactly 4 clusters, exactly 12 questions at 8-12). Quality strong through Q10, degrades to generic scalability padding at Q11-12. On strong triggers (e.g., wearable-device): zero filler, 50-60% distinctive contribution with genuine engineering tradeoffs. On weak triggers: 8-17% filler, 15-25% distinctive contribution with ~50-60% Analyst overlap. Paradigm-reinforcement problem worsens at higher volumes — engineering metaphors become a cognitive cage on non-technical topics (e.g., career-change “legacy code,” “system rewrites”). Clear false positive on career-change control. |

## Personas Excluded from Question Generation (4)

These personas should not be included in any question generations.

| Persona | Rationale | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Momentum Builder** | ~59% generative / 41% convergent question mix. Many questions embed implementation scaffolding — seed idea → elaboration → aspirational extension within a single question, reducing open-endedness. | Without live facilitation, long elaborative questions crowd the question space and bias downstream responses. Phase 3 is the natural home where progressive elaboration is the goal. |
| **Pragmatist** | Primarily convergent and feasibility-focused; its questions narrow territory. Low-volume, low-variance persona with focus on implementation, MVP, and realistic constraints. | If placed too early or overrepresented, prematurely converges the question space. Better as an answer persona in Phase 3 where it provides counterweight to Visionary and Provocateur. |
| **Simplifier** | **Fully excluded — no conditional retention.** ~60-65% of questions are convergent evaluations (“What if we removed X?”) that narrow rather than expand the possibility space. Cross-persona viability test on the Simplifier's strongest possible trigger topic (mobile-app) found 0 of 27 questions covering territory unaddressed by other personas. The Constraint Flipper covers simplification territory with a generative rather than convergent orientation (“What if this constraint is an advantage?” vs “What if we removed X?”). | ~50-60% overlap with Analyst (highest of any persona pair). Filler onset at ~9 questions — lowest ceiling of any persona tested. Youth-mentorship safety concern: subtractive lens misapplied to safeguards for vulnerable populations. Redirect strengths to Phase 3 scope assessment. |
| **Synthesizer** | Primary value is in Phase 4 synthesis rather than initial question generation. Question outputs lean heavily toward meta-process and convergent focusing rather than raw divergence. | Reserve for Phase 4 or special meta-question tasks (e.g., “what are we not asking?”) rather than standard Phase 2B question lists. Could be experimented with as a meta-question subagent to counter anchoring effects from early personas, but this is a distinct architectural role. |

**Connector** is a special case handled separately from the other Tier 3 personas. When selected by the orchestrator, it **replaces the Analogist** in the Tier 1 Perspective slot and inherits the 5-8 questions Append treatment. See [R7: Connector Volume-Quality](../../dev/2026-02-21_symphony-question-generation_part2/findings/R7_connector-volume-quality.md) for full analysis.

**Swap Analogist → Connector when:**
- The topic requires bridging, merging, or reconciling distinct systems that constrain each other
- The user's question is about connecting domains, not just understanding one domain better
- Examples: career transitions across fields, multi-system institutional change, cross-disciplinary technical design, social program design spanning multiple institutional systems

**Keep Analogist when:**
- The topic has a clear single primary domain
- The value of the Perspective persona is creative metaphorical reframing
- Examples: event planning, single-business strategy, single-product design, single-community initiatives

**Default: Analogist.** The Analogist is the safer choice across all topic types. The Connector adds distinctive value on ~50% of topics; on the remainder it either matches (ties) or falls back to import mode indistinguishable from the Analogist. The Connector inherits Analogist's append mechanics cleanly: same 3-cluster structure, same self-containment, same round-robin compatibility.

| Persona | Effort | Generate | Phase 2C Method | Selection Trigger |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Connector** | Medium, High | 5-8 | Append (replaces Analogist) | Inter-domain reconciliation: bridging systems that constrain each other |

## Topic Affinity Hints

Use these hints when choosing the non-core personas for medium and high effort sessions.

## Phase 2C Downstream Implications

Phase 2B persona selection affects how questions flow into Phase 2C synthesis. Personas fall into two groups based on how their questions should be handled downstream.

### Synthesize Group

These personas produce questions with **high thematic overlap** across shared concerns (monetization, user engagement, failure modes, metrics). Their questions should be cross-referenced and merged during Phase 2C synthesis.

| Persona | Tier | Overlap Pattern |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Questioner** | Tier 1 | Broad assumption-challenging questions overlap with many other personas' territory |
| **Analyst** | Tier 1 | Systematic coverage overlaps with Systems Thinker and Devil's Advocate on risk, data, metrics |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Tier 1 | Risk identification overlaps with Systems Thinker (failure modes) and Audience Advocate (equity risks) |
| **Audience Advocate** | Tier 1 | Stakeholder concerns overlap with Appreciative Inquirer (user experience) and Devil's Advocate (ethical risks) |
| **Systems Thinker** | Tier 1 | Feedback loop analysis overlaps with Analyst (metrics) and Devil's Advocate (failure modes) |
| **Appreciative Inquirer** | Tier 1 | Strengths-based framing overlaps with Audience Advocate (positive user experience) and Questioner (vision/values) |
| **Empath** | Tier 2 | ~25-30% convergence with Storyteller, ~15-20% with Audience Advocate; meta-cognitive Qs should be preserved |
| **First Principles Thinker** | Tier 2 | ~43-47% overlap with Questioner; self-answering tendency benefits from synthesis pruning |
| **Futurist** | Tier 2 | Strategic-positioning questions overlap with Tier 1 Structural/Analytical; temporal decoration naturally pruned |
| **Accountant** | Tier 3 | ~40-50% overlap with Analyst at different depth (Analyst creates scaffolding, Accountant fills with data) |

### Append Group

These personas produce questions with **highly distinctive framing** that would be diluted or lost through synthesis. Their questions should be directly appended to the synthesized output.

| Persona | Tier | Why Append | Recommended Append Count |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Provocateur** | Tier 1 | Absurd inversion + "real insight" format is the entire value. Synthesizing strips the provocation. | 5-6 |

## Quality Degradation Patterns

When adjusting volume targets, be aware of these consistent degradation patterns that emerge beyond a persona's recommended range:

1. **Thematic repetition** — same core concern rephrased with slightly different framing
2. **Dimensional drift** — persona addresses topics outside its natural lens, losing distinctiveness
3. **Granularity inflation** — broad, impactful questions fragment into narrow sub-questions
4. **Formulaic voice** — the persona's distinctive framing (narrative, inversion, metaphor) becomes a template applied mechanically

### Volume Overshoot

Several personas show topic-complexity-correlated overshooting where rich multi-stakeholder or multi-regulatory topics push Analytical and Structural personas past their volume ceilings. Quality remains high in overshooting runs, so this is an implementation consideration (prompt enforcement, post-hoc truncation) rather than a quality concern. The Questioner is the worst offender (40% overshoot rate). Self-reported question counts should not be trusted — post-hoc validation of actual counts is recommended.

### Volume Floor

The minimum useful volume is **8 questions** for standard question generation. Below 8, the model shifts from generating a list of focused questions to producing compound "research brief" style questions that pack multiple concerns into each item. These compound questions are too dense for downstream synthesis or brainstorming.
