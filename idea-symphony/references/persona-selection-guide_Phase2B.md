# Persona Selection Guide (Phase 2B)

This document provides the orchestrator with structured guidance for selecting question-generation personas and setting per-persona volume targets in Phase 2B.

---

## Tier 1 Question Personas (10)

Phase 2B draws from a pool of 10 personas validated for question generation. 

### Per-Persona Guidance

| Persona | Guidance | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Questioner** | Include in nearly all rosters as the meta-question generator to widen problem framing before more specialized personas fire. Pair with Devil's Advocate and a human-centered persona (Storyteller or Audience Advocate) to turn analytical probes into lived-experience scenarios. | Dimensional profile ~49% analytical, 39% human-centered, 28% provocative. Native question persona; high volume, multi-dimensional. Very high selflessness — many questions explicitly open territory for other personas. Low convergence (~13%) with First Principles Thinker despite both being analytical. |
| **Analyst** | Include whenever analytical coverage is desired, especially alongside Devil's Advocate. Combine with a generative persona (Visionary or Analogist) to approximate full CPS Clarify-Ideate-Develop coverage. | Dimensional profile ~71% analytical with strategic and mild systems coverage; zero provocative questions. Highly selfless — any Phase 3 persona can answer. Clean 5-cluster outputs with low variance (CV~9.7%). Complementary with (not redundant to) Devil's Advocate — no direct cluster overlap. Risk of analytical bias arises only if paired exclusively with other analytical/evaluative personas with no generative counterweights. |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Use in all but the most lighthearted sessions. Pair with Appreciative Inquirer or Visionary to maintain balance between risk discovery and aspirational exploration, countering framing bias. | Dimensional profile ~53% provocative with strong human-centered and tactical sub-dimensions; only ~2% analytical. Topic-adaptive — volume expands on complex topics with many "attack surfaces." Functions as Six Hats Black Hat; must be deliberately balanced by generative personas to avoid pessimistic anchoring. |
| **Audience Advocate** | Include when user explicitly cares about marginalized stakeholders, accessibility, or real-world community impacts. Pair with Analyst (for metrics and frameworks) and Storyteller (for concrete user journeys) to turn structural concerns into rich question sets. | Dimensional profile ~60% human-centered, ~20% systems. Structurally-aware human-centeredness unique among human-centered personas — questions about documentation barriers, equity frameworks, and systemic inclusion open territory for analytical, systems, and practical personas. Higher selflessness than Empath (60% vs 33%). Low overlap with Storyteller (~12%) and Appreciative Inquirer (~11%). Cross-volume stable — equity lens recognizable even at low volumes. |
| **Systems Thinker** | Include when the topic is infrastructural, policy, or ecosystem-level. Pair with Visionary/Analogist and Devil's Advocate for a full range of system futures and risks. Without generative partners, can skew toward diagnostic rather than imaginative questioning. | Dimensional profile ~50% systems, ~21% human-centered. Named system archetypes (Fixes that Fail, Shifting the Burden, etc.) are unique vocabulary across all personas. Fills the single most underrepresented dimension in the roster. Exceptional consistency (CV=2.2%). ~56-60% unique territory vs Tier 1 aggregate. HIGH selflessness (~60-65%). |
| **Appreciative Inquirer** | Include whenever there is risk that analytical or risk personas will dominate framing. Pair with Devil's Advocate and Analyst to create a dual lens of "what's strong" and "what can break." Cannot function as standalone question generator — deficit-avoidance is complete (zero risk/failure questions). | ~39% strict strengths-based questions (3-6x the rate of any other persona). Dimension profile nearly inverse of the analytical set — strong Creative (26%) and Strategic (18%) where the set is weak. ~70% selflessness. Very low topic sensitivity — 4-D structure stable across all topic types. ~18% convergence with Visionary, ~22% with Empath, ~12% with Audience Advocate. |
| **Provocateur** | Use when the risk of fixation is high or when the user explicitly wants non-obvious, edgy perspectives. Always pair with at least one strong analytical persona and one human-centered persona to ensure provocations are grounded and ethically framed. | Dimensional profile ~52% provocative with creative as frequent secondary. Cluster structures uniquely subversive (e.g., "The Anti-Library"). Extremely powerful lateral disruptor. Strongest on abstract topics where there are more assumptions to challenge; slightly weaker on concrete topics where provocations can feel disconnected from practical constraints. |
| **Analogist** | Use alongside Visionary and Provocateur to provide multiple distinct paths to divergence: reimagination (Visionary), provocation (Provocateur), and analogy (Analogist). For highly technical or regulated domains, pair with a grounding persona to ensure imported analogies respect real constraints. | Distinctive cluster structures organized by source domain (biological, historical, industry). Introduces conceptual frameworks (mycorrhizal networks, desire lines, keystone habits) no analytical persona generates. Somewhat "selfish" — best positioned to answer its own questions — but imported frameworks create scaffolding other personas can build on. Reliable volume (CV=10.1%). Finds richer metaphors for complex topics than for concrete ones. |
| **Visionary** | Include when the task calls for reimagining categories (e.g., new products, systems redesign). Pair with Analyst and Systems Thinker for grounding. Combine with Appreciative Inquirer to connect aspirational futures to existing strengths rather than pure blue-sky. | Operates via reconstructive aspiration, not destructive challenge — complementary to Provocateur. Clean generative persona; high selflessness. Blank-slate reimagination distinct from Appreciative Inquirer's build-from-strengths (~18% convergence). Quality varies by topic — stronger on abstract/digital topics than concrete/time-bounded events. |
| **Storyteller** | Include whenever user experience, engagement, or emotional journey matters, or when countering an overly analytical question set. Pair with Audience Advocate and Empath for rich, multi-perspective human-centered question spaces, and with Systems Thinker to connect micro-stories to macro-patterns. | Most distinctive questions of all Perspective personas — narrative-arc clusters (Arrival, Hero's Journey, etc.) no analytical persona approximates. Highest selflessness among Perspective tier — narrative scenarios invite Empath, Visionary, Pragmatist, Audience Advocate responses. Topic-sensitive — expands for topics with richer human characters (community > product > event). |

### Persona Categories

Personas are organized into three categories based on how their question quality scales with volume.

| Category | Personas | Question Range | Rationale |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Perspective** (narrow lens, distinctive voice) | Provocateur, Analogist, Visionary, Storyteller | **5-8** | Voice becomes formulaic beyond 8; value is in concentrated insight |
| **Structural** (systematic coverage through a framework) | Systems Thinker, Audience Advocate, Devil's Advocate, Appreciative Inquirer | **8-12** | Framework provides natural structure; ~3 questions per cluster |
| **Analytical** (breadth-seeking, systematic coverage) | Questioner, Analyst | **12-15** | Thrives on volume; uses additional questions for genuine new territory |

### Per-Persona Volume Recommendations

| Persona | Category | Recommended Range | Filler Onset | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Questioner** | Analytical | 12-20 | ~18 Qs | Only persona that genuinely benefits from 15-20. Even at 20, <5% filler. |
| **Analyst** | Analytical | 10-15 | ~15 Qs | Strong systematic coverage. Fragments into narrow sub-questions beyond 15. |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Structural | 8-12 | ~12 Qs | Most impactful focused on structural flaws. Beyond 12, shifts from vulnerabilities to minor operational concerns. |
| **Audience Advocate** | Structural | 10-15 | ~13 Qs | Many distinct stakeholder dimensions justify higher end of Structural range. Lowest filler of any Structural persona. |
| **Systems Thinker** | Structural | 10-15 | ~12 Qs | Enough distinct archetypes and subsystems. Becomes pedantic beyond 15. Quality strongest on complex multi-element topics. |
| **Appreciative Inquirer** | Structural | 8-12 | ~12 Qs | 4-phase AI framework (Discovery, Dream, Design, Destiny) provides natural boundaries. 2-3 questions per phase is optimal. |
| **Provocateur** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~8 Qs | Concentrated burst of assumption-breaking. Beyond 8, provocations become formulaic and shock value diminishes. Highest filler rate (30-40%) at 15-20. |
| **Analogist** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~10 Qs | A few powerful reframing metaphors, not an exhaustive catalogue. Quality drops sharply beyond 10 (surface-level parallels). |
| **Visionary** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~10 Qs | Most powerful when focused on a few genuinely transformative reframings. Often undershoots high targets naturally. |
| **Storyteller** | Perspective | 5-8 | ~8 Qs | Value is in emotional resonance and user journey illumination. Narrative framing becomes forced beyond 8. |

## Tier 2 Question Personas (5)

These personas are not typically included by default in medium effort question generations. Based on selection guidance TBD and decision-making TBD on effort settings, their questions may be added into high effort question generations to bring in new approaches or compliment Tier 1 perspectives.

| Persona | Guidance | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Constraint Flipper** | Include as a secondary generative persona for topics where constraints are heavily felt (tight budgets, regulations, physical limitations). | Genuine constraint-inversion operation — asks “how is this limitation actually better?” rather than “how do we work within this?” Produces 14-17 questions consistently (CV=3.7%), notably lower volume than other generative personas. The inversion operation is powerful but exhaustible — once each major constraint is flipped, the persona runs out of moves. Underlying constraints it identifies (budget, time, space) overlap with analytical personas; the *direction* of the question is what’s distinctive. Conceptually close to Lateral Thinking “challenge” technique; particularly helpful against problem-framed prompts that might otherwise induce defensive analysis. Lowest selflessness of the generative tier — questions mostly require its own reframing skill to answer fully. |
| **Empath** | Add when emotional resonance and psychological safety are central concerns, such as wellness, education, or sensitive community work. | Operates inside-out via emotional excavation — “how does it feel?” vs Audience Advocate’s outside-in “how might we design for?” Dimensional profile ~75% human-centered, ~10% creative. Extreme consistency across topics (CV=1.4%). Unique territory includes parental emotional subtext, shame/worthiness dynamics, emotional labor sustainability, and meta-cognitive questions (“What does this app believe about human nature?”). ~25-30% convergence with Storyteller (both care about emotional experience but through different entry points — felt states vs narrative arcs). Lower selflessness than Audience Advocate (33% vs 60%) — most Empath questions channel through human-centered Phase 3 personas. Distinctiveness degrades at lower volumes, becoming harder to distinguish from Storyteller and Appreciative Inquirer. Best in combination with Storyteller and Audience Advocate, where Empath’s gut signals can seed deeper narrative and equity questions. |
| **First Principles Thinker** | Add when high-effort rosters allow more analytical bandwidth or when domain complexity demands deep structural modeling (e.g., data architecture, behavior change mechanics). | Ontological/definitional cognitive mode — “What IS this, fundamentally?” and “What are the necessary and sufficient conditions?” Dimensional profile ~87% analytical with ~27% systems secondary. ~40-45% overlap with Questioner (both challenge assumptions, but FPT uses rigorous reductionism vs Questioner’s broader curiosity). ~35-40% unique territory vs Tier 1 aggregate — lower than Systems Thinker’s ~56-60%. Many questions are self-answering, reducing cross-roster selflessness (~45-50%) relative to Questioner. Core contribution — rigorous ontological questioning — could partially be achieved through prompt engineering to existing analytical personas; Systems Thinker’s dynamic analysis is harder to replicate without a dedicated persona. |
| **Futurist** | Include when topic is civic, infrastructural, or technical; omit for purely personal/experiential contexts. | Cognitive mode is trend extrapolation, not creative reimagination — dimensional profile 56% Strategic, 21% Analytical, 0% Creative. Despite “forward-looking” branding, operates as a strategic persona, not a Visionary alternative. ~30-35% thematic convergence with Visionary but treatment is consistently different (strategic positioning vs paradigm reimagination). ~29% temporal lens anti-pattern rate overall (41% on experiential topics, ~22% on civic/technical) — questions that are standard planning questions with trend decoration bolted on. Moderate selflessness — many questions invite trend-based answers rather than diverse perspectives. Strongest on civic and technical topics where external trends genuinely shape the project. Most structurally consistent persona evaluated (CV=4.4%). |

## Tier 3 Question Personas (5)

These personas are not typically included by default in any question generations. Based on selection guidance TBD, like Tier 1 Perspective personas, their questions may appended to medium and high effort question generations when the orchestrator identifies a specific need to include domain-specific questions.

| Persona | Guidance | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Accountant** | Include only for topics where financial structure is central (business models, funding mechanisms, community budgeting). Topic keywords that increase selection priority: “business,” “nonprofit,” “budget,” “startup,” “pricing,” “revenue.” | Tier 1 financial coverage is 18.4% overall but highly uneven — adequate for community topics (~30%), significant gap for product/business topics (~10.5%). The Analyst carries 61% of Tier 1’s financial questions but stops at framework-level analysis. The Accountant picks up where the Analyst leaves off: quantified benchmarks (dollar ranges, percentages), depreciation/asset lifecycle, reserve fund structuring, insurance categories, unit economics chains with actual numbers, opportunity cost quantification. Complementary with Analyst — Analyst creates analytical structure, Accountant fills it with financial data and modeling. Pair with Appreciative Inquirer or Visionary so economic questions do not overconstrain innovation. |
| **Connector** | Use when the topic spans multiple domains that must be reconciled (e.g., UX + sustainability + business model). Do NOT include alongside Analogist at medium effort — choose one. | Operates in three distinct sub-modes: cross-domain import (~35%, overlaps with Analogist), structural isomorphism (~30%, unique — “how are X and Y actually the same problem?”), and recombinant innovation (~35%, unique — “what if we combined X + Y?”). ~30-40% overlap with Analogist concentrated in the import sub-mode; isomorphism and recombination are genuinely distinct. Distinctiveness vs Systems Thinker is HIGH (~80-85%) — different cognitive operations despite vocabulary overlap. Topic-sensitive (CV=17.5%) — recombinant mode strongest on conceptually complex topics, falls back to Analogist-like import on simpler topics. Moderate selflessness overall; recombinant questions are among the most selfless in the corpus. Likely adds most value in Phase 3 and 4; Phase 2B can often get similar benefits from Analogist + Systems Thinker. |
| **Lawyer** | Add only when legal/regulatory exposure is part of the user’s explicit concern; otherwise, let Devil’s Advocate flag high-level risk. | Produces more clusters and the largest average file size of any persona, often 7-8 clusters heavy with legal framing. Clusters over-structure the space with numerous compliance slices, risking cognitive overload for synthesis. Strongly evaluative and problem-framed — high specificity but heavy negative framing and low generative expansion. Critical for regulated or liability-heavy topics. Acts like an extreme Black Hat; given anchoring risk, questions should be intentionally mixed with appreciative and generative ones in synthesis to avoid defensive ideation. |
| **Politician** | Deploy for civic, institutional, or organizational change topics; unnecessary for personal or purely technical questions. | Only high-variance persona (CV~30.3%) — produces double output for institutional topics vs personal topics, indicating strong topic sensitivity. Clusters around power, governance, coalition-building; territory distinct from Analyst and Devil’s Advocate. Highly valuable but only when stakeholder politics are central. Cannot enact process design but can still generate governance and coalition questions that other personas can then answer. |
| **Simplifier** | Use only when simplification is explicitly part of the user’s stated goal (e.g., de-scoping a bloated system). | Strongly convergent via subtraction — “what if we removed this?” Low-volume persona with consistent 4-5 cluster outputs focused on removal and essentialization. Rarely generates pure generative what-if questions; most are “what if we did not…?” evaluations. Low open-endedness and modest imaginative scope; can prematurely collapse solution space. Better as Phase 3 answerer than Phase 2B core generator. When used in Phase 2B, must be combined with strong generative personas to avoid net subtractive bias. |
| **Technical Expert** | Add when user prompts highlight specific technical unknowns (“how could this be built?”) and when risk of speculative architecture is high. | Low volume with narrow clusters around architecture, dependencies, and technical risk. Reinforces existing implementation paradigms — reflects the research warning that expert-driven question generation can entrench current assumptions. High specificity and constraint but low imaginative breadth. Generally better as a Phase 3 answer persona or a topic-gated question generator. |

## Personas Excluded from Question Generation (3)

These personas should not be included in any question generations.

| Persona | Rationale | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Momentum Builder** | ~59% generative / 41% convergent question mix. Many questions embed implementation scaffolding — seed idea → elaboration → aspirational extension within a single question, reducing open-endedness. | Without live facilitation, long elaborative questions crowd the question space and bias downstream responses. Phase 3 is the natural home where progressive elaboration is the goal. |
| **Pragmatist** | Primarily convergent and feasibility-focused; its questions narrow territory. Low-volume, low-variance persona with focus on implementation, MVP, and realistic constraints. | If placed too early or overrepresented, prematurely converges the question space. Better as an answer persona in Phase 3 where it provides counterweight to Visionary and Provocateur. |
| **Synthesizer** | Primary value is in Phase 4 synthesis rather than initial question generation. Question outputs lean heavily toward meta-process and convergent focusing rather than raw divergence. | Reserve for Phase 4 or special meta-question tasks (e.g., “what are we not asking?”) rather than standard Phase 2B question lists. Could be experimented with as a meta-question subagent to counter anchoring effects from early personas, but this is a distinct architectural role. |

Structural personas (**Devil's Advocate**, **Audience Advocate**, **Systems Thinker**, **Appreciative Inquirer**) generate a variable number of questions in Phase 2B depending on effort. ### Tier 1 Perspective Personas (Appended)

Perspective personas (**Provocateur**, **Analogist**, **Visionary**, **Storyteller**) generate a fixed range of questions (5-8) in Phase 2B regardless of effort. In Phase 2C, the orchestrator appends a subset by round-robin cluster selection rather than including all questions or crafting prompts for exact output counts. See [D1: Perspective Persona Append Strategy](../../dev/2026-02-21_symphony-question-generation_part2/findings/D1_perspective-persona-append-strategy.md) for full analysis.

| Personas | Effort | Generate | Phase 2C Method | Append per Persona | Selection Method |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Provocateur, Analogist, Visionary, Storyteller | Low | 5-8 | Append | 3 | One per cluster, round-robin |
| Provocateur, Analogist, Visionary, Storyteller | Medium | 5-8 | Append | 5 | One per cluster, then fill from largest clusters |
| Provocateur, Analogist, Visionary, Storyteller | High | 5-8 | Append | All (5-8) | No selection needed — append everything |

### Tier 2 Question Personas (Synthesized)

Tier 2 question personas (**Constraint Flipper**, **Empath**, **First Principles Thinker**, **Futurist**) generate TBD questions in Phase 2B only on **high effort**. In Phase 2C, the orchestrator includes all questions from these Tier 2 personas in the synthesis step for high effort runs. See [R5: Tier 2 Volume-Quality](../../dev/2026-02-21_symphony-question-generation_part2/tasks/R5_tier2-volume-quality.md) for pending investigation.

### Tier 3 Question Personas (Mixed Methods)

Tier 3 question personas (**Accountant**, **Lawyer**, **Politician**, **Simplifier**, **Technical Expert**) generate TBD questions each in Phase 2B, only on **medium or high effort** and only when **specifically selected by the orchestrator** for inclusion. In Phase 2C, the orchestrator includes questions from these selected Tier 3 personas by synthesis or append for medium and high effort runs. See [R6: Tier 3 Volume-Quality](../../dev/2026-02-21_symphony-question-generation_part2/tasks/R6_tier3-volume-quality.md) for pending investigation.

**Connector** is a special case handled separately from the other Tier 3 personas. If selected by the orchestrator, it is a **replacement for Analogist** in the Tier 1 Perspective Personas and would inherit the Append treatment from D1. See [R7: Connector Volume-Quality](../../dev/2026-02-21_symphony-question-generation_part2/tasks/R7_connector-volume-quality.md) for pending investigation.

## Topic Affinity Hints

Use these hints when choosing the non-core personas for medium and high effort sessions.

| Topic Characteristics | Recommended Personas | Avoid |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Complex systems, many interacting elements** | Systems Thinker, Analyst | Storyteller (narrative framing adds less on systems topics) |
| **User-facing, community, service design** | Audience Advocate, Storyteller, Appreciative Inquirer | — |
| **Abstract, strategic, innovation-focused** | Visionary, Provocateur | Appreciative Inquirer (strengths-based framing less useful in blue-sky) |
| **Entrenched assumptions, "always done it this way"** | Devil's Advocate, Provocateur | — |
| **Novel/ambiguous, cross-domain** | Analogist, Systems Thinker | — |
| **Concrete, time-bounded event** | Appreciative Inquirer, Audience Advocate | Visionary (less paradigm-shifting space on concrete topics) |

## Phase 2C Downstream Implications

Phase 2B persona selection affects how questions flow into Phase 2C synthesis. Personas fall into two groups based on how their questions should be handled downstream.

### Synthesize Group

These personas produce questions with **high thematic overlap** across shared concerns (monetization, user engagement, failure modes, metrics). Their questions should be cross-referenced and merged during Phase 2C synthesis.

| Persona | Overlap Pattern |
|:---|:---|
| **Questioner** | Broad assumption-challenging questions overlap with many other personas' territory |
| **Analyst** | Systematic coverage overlaps with Systems Thinker and Devil's Advocate on risk, data, metrics |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Risk identification overlaps with Systems Thinker (failure modes) and Audience Advocate (equity risks) |
| **Audience Advocate** | Stakeholder concerns overlap with Appreciative Inquirer (user experience) and Devil's Advocate (ethical risks) |
| **Systems Thinker** | Feedback loop analysis overlaps with Analyst (metrics) and Devil's Advocate (failure modes) |
| **Appreciative Inquirer** | Strengths-based framing overlaps with Audience Advocate (positive user experience) and Questioner (vision/values) |

### Append Group

These personas produce questions with **highly distinctive framing** that would be diluted or lost through synthesis. Their questions should be directly appended to the synthesized output at 5-8 questions each.

| Persona | Why Append | Recommended Append Count |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Provocateur** | Absurd inversion + "real insight" format is the entire value. Synthesizing strips the provocation. | 5-6 |
| **Analogist** | Cross-domain metaphors are specific and vivid. Synthesizing loses the reframing power. | 5-8 |
| **Visionary** | Paradigm-challenging questions exist in a different register. Synthesis domesticates them. | 5-6 |
| **Storyteller** | Narrative vignettes and character-driven scenarios are their own form. Synthesis loses narrative specificity. | 5-6 |

### Expected Phase 2C Output

When using persona-specific volume targets:
- **Synthesize group** produces ~50-70 questions (depending on effort level) that synthesize down to ~20-30 thematic clusters
- **Append group** contributes ~20-24 distinctive questions passed through directly
- **Total Phase 2C output:** ~45-55 questions (synthesized clusters + appended distinctive questions)

## Quality Degradation Patterns

When adjusting volume targets, be aware of these consistent degradation patterns that emerge beyond a persona's recommended range:

1. **Thematic repetition** — same core concern rephrased with slightly different framing
2. **Dimensional drift** — persona addresses topics outside its natural lens, losing distinctiveness
3. **Granularity inflation** — broad, impactful questions fragment into narrow sub-questions
4. **Formulaic voice** — the persona's distinctive framing (narrative, inversion, metaphor) becomes a template applied mechanically

### Volume Floor

The minimum useful volume is **8 questions** for standard question generation. Below 8, the model shifts from generating a list of focused questions to producing compound "research brief" style questions that pack multiple concerns into each item. These compound questions are too dense for downstream synthesis or brainstorming.
