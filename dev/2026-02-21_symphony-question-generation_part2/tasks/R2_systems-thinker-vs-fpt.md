## Background

### Context

The Tier 1 roster (medium effort, 8 personas) is established with strong analytical coverage:
- Questioner (broad versatile analyst)
- Analyst (systematic decomposition)
- Devil's Advocate (critical/challenging perspective)

**Question:** Should the 9th persona be an additional analytical/systems thinker? If so, which one?

Both Systems Thinker and First Principles Thinker are classified as analytical/systems personas. Prior research has:
- I06: Identified "Systems" as a dimension at only 6.5% in the original Phase 2B set, with FPT as primary contributor
- I12: Validated Systems Thinker as a capable new persona
- Persona coverage mapping: Rated both as "Medium-high" for question generation capacity

### Key Differences

**Systems Thinker:** Focuses on feedback loops, interdependencies, second-order effects, emergence, and dynamic complexity. Primary mode is tracing causal chains and recognizing patterns of behavior.

**First Principles Thinker:** Focuses on ontological decomposition, minimal conditions, mechanistic analysis, and foundational assumptions. Primary mode is breaking things down to irreducible elements.

**Existing Tier 1 analytical coverage:**
- Questioner: broad question generation across all modes (most versatile)
- Analyst: systematic breakdown, structured analysis, logical dependencies
- Devil's Advocate: critical examination, assumption challenge, risk identification

### Research Questions

1. **RQ9a Unique territory:** What questions does each candidate generate that NO Tier 1 persona covers?
2. **RQ9b Questioner overlap:** Given Questioner is the most versatile analytical persona, how much does each candidate duplicate its coverage?
3. **RQ9c Analyst overlap:** Given Analyst does systematic decomposition, how much does each candidate overlap with it?
4. **RQ9d Systems dimension:** Does the Systems Thinker significantly increase Systems-dimension coverage beyond what the existing Tier 1 set provides?
5. **RQ9e Cognitive operation distinction:** Are internal dynamics (ST) vs structural decomposition (FPT) genuinely different question types, or do they converge?
6. **RQ9f Volume and efficiency:** ST produces ~50% more content per topic — is this additional volume valuable or does it include filler?
7. **RQ9g Selflessness:** Do the questions open territory for diverse Phase 3 personas or primarily for analytical answerers?

**Comparison criteria:**

1. **Unique territory:** What questions does each candidate generate that NO Tier 1 persona covers? Look for themes, causal reasoning styles, and analytical modes not present in the 8-persona aggregate.

2. **Overlap with Questioner:** Questioner is the most versatile analytical persona. Count questions from each candidate that overlap with Questioner's broad question generation. Calculate convergence %.

3. **Overlap with Analyst:** Analyst does systematic decomposition. Count questions from each candidate that overlap with Analyst's structured breakdown. Calculate convergence %.

4. **Systems dimension contribution:** Using the Systems dimension classification from I06, count how many questions from each candidate fall into the Systems dimension. Compare to the original set's 6.5% Systems coverage. Does each candidate significantly increase Systems-dimension questions?

5. **Cognitive operation distinction:** Classify questions by cognitive mode:
   - Systems Thinker: feedback loops, causal chains, second-order effects, emergence patterns
   - First Principles Thinker: decomposition, reductionist analysis, foundational assumptions, minimal conditions
   - Calculate % questions in each mode. If the two candidates have >70% overlap in classification, they may be too similar.

6. **Volume and efficiency:** ST produces ~31 KB (50% more content than FPT's 20 KB). Assess whether the additional volume yields proportional question diversity or contains redundancy/filler.

7. **Selflessness:** For each candidate's questions, identify which Phase 3 personas could productively answer it. High-value Phase 2B questions open territory for diverse personas. Calculate % selfless questions (answerable by 3+ personas from different clusters).
