## Quality Assessment

Quality across the tested range is uniformly strong. Every question anchors in a specific, well-developed analogy with genuine structural parallels to the topic. Key quality indicators:

**Analogy depth:** Nearly all analogies draw structural parallels, not surface similarities. The career-change topic's "technical debt" metaphor (nursing experience as both asset and constraint) and the school-consolidation topic's "river confluence" metaphor (distinct currents persisting for miles before blending) are representative of the level of development. These are not "X is like Y" observations but extended explorations of how the source domain's dynamics illuminate the target domain.

**Source domain diversity:** Across 10 topics, the Analogist draws from biology/ecology, history, business models, technology systems, architecture, performing arts, natural phenomena, institutional design, and more. Within each topic, the 3 clusters typically span distinctly different source domain families (e.g., school-consolidation uses natural systems, historical parallels, and metaphorical reframing).

**Actionability:** Questions consistently push toward concrete implications rather than abstract comparison. "What would a school district look like if it adopted a franchise model?" is immediately actionable. "How might mycorrhizal networks inform mentor matching?" leads directly to specific matching criteria.

**No filler detected at this range.** Every question across all 10 topics introduces a genuinely distinct analogy that adds a new perspective. There is no padding or recycling of metaphors within or across clusters.

## Filler Assessment

V1 take 2 estimated filler onset at ~10 questions, with 25-35% filler at 15-20. This validation tested at 5-8, which sits entirely below the predicted filler threshold. As expected, zero filler was observed at this range across all topics.

This is consistent with V1 take 2's finding that the Analogist's "core paradigm-shifting metaphors" occupy roughly the first 5-10 questions. The stabilized 5-8 bucket captures the sweet spot: enough metaphors to cover diverse source domains without stretching into forced or superficial analogies.

The stabilized range effectively eliminates the filler problem described in V1 take 2 by staying well within the quality ceiling.

## Cross-Topic Consistency

V1 take 2 noted that the Analogist "finds richer metaphors for complex topics (habit-tracker) than for concrete ones (space-party), where the analogies sometimes feel forced." This finding requires nuance in light of the new data:

**Space-party (the predicted weak case):** The Analogist actually performs well here at the 5-8 range. The metaphors (museum learning loops, theme park narrative arcs, flight mission operations, theater set design, biological swarm coordination) are genuinely useful for party planning. At this constrained volume, the Analogist avoids the "strained" analogies that V1 take 2 predicted for concrete topics. This suggests the earlier observation about space-party weakness was a volume effect, not a topic effect -- at 15-20 questions the Analogist would run out of good party metaphors, but at 8 they are all solid.

**Complex topics remain strong:** Career-change, school-consolidation, wearable-device, and mobile-app all produce exceptionally rich analogies. The school-consolidation output is particularly impressive, with the "river confluence," "franchise model," "rural post office," and "hospital merger wave" analogies each illuminating a genuinely different dimension of the consolidation challenge.

**Concrete/operational topics:** Food-truck and property-management produce slightly more conventional analogies (hub-and-spoke logistics, craft brewery expansion playbook), but these are still structurally sound and useful. The property-management topic's "certified organic label" analogy is genuinely creative for the STR regulatory context.

**Community/social topics:** Tool-library and youth-mentorship both generate strong ecological and institutional analogies. The youth-mentorship output draws particularly well from hospice volunteer training and AA group structures -- source domains that are non-obvious and structurally illuminating.

**Overall:** Cross-topic consistency at 5-8 questions is significantly better than V1 take 2 suggested for the persona's full output range. The stabilized volume eliminates the topic-dependent quality variance that appeared at higher volumes.

## Append Strategy Assessment

**Cluster formation:** All 10 topics produce exactly 3 clusters, with cluster sizes ranging from 2-3 questions each. This extreme consistency makes round-robin selection straightforward and predictable.

**Round-robin at 3 questions (LOW effort):** Selecting one question per cluster would reliably capture three distinct source-domain families per topic. Because the Analogist consistently organizes clusters by source domain type (e.g., natural systems / cross-industry parallels / metaphorical reframing), round-robin guarantees perspective diversity. This works well.

**Round-robin at 5 questions (MEDIUM effort):** One per cluster (3) plus two from the largest clusters. Given cluster sizes of 2-3, this means the largest cluster contributes 2-3 questions. Since questions within a cluster draw from different specific analogies (e.g., "mycorrhizal networks" and "river confluences" are both natural-system analogies but illuminate different aspects), within-cluster selections remain non-redundant. This works well.

**Full 5-8 at HIGH effort:** All questions appended. No filler concern at this range. Works well.

**Individual question independence:** Questions are fully self-contained. Each one names its source domain, develops the structural parallel, and poses a specific question. No question depends on another for context. Round-robin selection can pick any individual question without loss.

**Distinctive voice preservation:** The Analogist's voice -- the cross-domain metaphor as question frame -- is unmistakable in every single question across all 10 topics. Even a single selected question immediately signals "this is the Analogist's contribution." The persona's value is not diluted by selection.

**One minor structural note:** The youth-mentorship output has a numbering reset within the file (questions in clusters 2 and 3 restart at 1 rather than continuing from 4). This is a formatting inconsistency, not a content issue, and does not affect selection logic.

## Notable Deviations

**Better than expected on concrete topics:** V1 take 2 predicted weaker performance on concrete topics like space-party. At 5-8 questions, this weakness does not manifest. The stabilized volume eliminates the predicted topic-type sensitivity.

**Mycorrhizal network repetition across topics:** The mycorrhizal/fungal network analogy appears in habit-tracker (Q3), school-consolidation (Q1), tool-library (Q1), and youth-mentorship (Q1). While each application explores a different structural parallel (resource sharing in forests applied to social habit tracking vs. district service sharing vs. tool circulation vs. mentor matching), this is a notable cross-topic pattern. Within any single topic, the analogy is used once and well-developed. But if the Analogist's output from multiple topics were ever combined or compared, the repeated source domain would be visible. This does not affect the per-topic quality assessment but is worth noting as a characteristic of the persona's analogy "vocabulary."

**Cluster count perfectly stable:** V1 take 2 did not specifically comment on cluster count stability. All 10 topics produce exactly 3 clusters. This is unusually consistent compared to what other personas typically show and makes the append strategy particularly predictable and reliable.

**Property-management and habit-tracker at 6 questions:** These two topics came in at the low end of the range. Both are still within spec (5-8), and the quality of all 6 questions in each is high. The slightly lower count may reflect the Analogist self-editing to avoid weaker analogies -- which is exactly the behavior that makes the 5-8 range work.
