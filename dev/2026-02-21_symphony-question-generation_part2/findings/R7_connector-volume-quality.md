### Quality Floor

The Connector matches the Analogist's zero-filler standard. Every question across all 10 topics introduces a genuinely distinct perspective. The Connector achieves this through a different mechanism than the Analogist: where the Analogist draws from varied source domains (biology, history, business), the Connector draws from varied cognitive operations (importing frameworks, identifying structural equivalences, proposing combinations). Both approaches produce questions that feel non-redundant within each topic.

The Connector's questions are consistently longer and more internally developed than the Analogist's. Connector questions average 2-3 sentences of setup before the question itself, often developing the structural parallel explicitly rather than leaving it as suggestion. This is neither better nor worse for Phase 2B purposes — both approaches produce actionable questions for Phase 3 brainstorming — but it does mean the Connector's output is wordier per question.

## Sub-Mode Distribution

Each Connector question was classified into one of three sub-modes:

- **Import** — brings a metaphor, model, or framework from another domain to illuminate the topic. Same cognitive operation as the Analogist.
- **Isomorphism** — identifies that two domains share the same underlying structure, problem shape, or dynamic. Goes beyond "X is like Y" to "X and Y are actually the same problem."
- **Recombination** — proposes combining, merging, or connecting elements from different domains to create something new. Most "selfless" mode — focused on the topic rather than the analogy.

### Distribution Analysis

The overall distribution (Import 43%, Isomorphism 31%, Recombination 26%) is close to the predicted split (35/30/35) from the selection guide, with import running slightly higher and recombination slightly lower.

**Critical finding: The distribution is not uniform across topics.** The topic-dependent variability is dramatic:

**Import-dominant topics (>50% import):**
- **space-party: 100% import.** This is the most significant finding. On a simple, concrete, single-domain topic, the Connector produces exclusively import-mode questions — functioning as a pure Analogist clone. Zero isomorphism, zero recombination. The Connector's distinctive cognitive modes do not activate at all.
- **tool-library: 63% import.** Majority import, with some recombination around connecting the tool library to other community systems.

**Balanced/distinctive topics (<40% import):**
- **career-change: 25% import.** Strong isomorphism (identifying structural parallels between nursing and law) and strong recombination (proposing hybrid career paths).
- **school-consolidation: 25% import.** Strongest recombination of any topic (4 of 8 questions). The multi-stakeholder, multi-domain nature of the topic strongly activates the recombinant mode.
- **wearable-device: 29% import.** Strong isomorphism (privacy constraint = power constraint, regulatory pathway parallels).
- **youth-mentorship: 29% import.** Strongest isomorphism of any topic (5 of 7 questions). The social-systems nature of the topic activates structural equivalence identification.

**Middle-ground topics (38-43% import):**
- food-truck, habit-tracker, mobile-app, property-management all cluster around 38-43% import with moderate isomorphism and recombination.

**The predicted topic-sensitivity (CV=17.5% from the selection guide) is confirmed and appears to be even more extreme than estimated.** The space-party result (100% import) represents a complete mode collapse.

## Cluster-Mode Alignment

### Cluster Structure

The Connector produces exactly 3 clusters per topic across all 10 topics, matching the Analogist's perfect consistency. Cluster sizes range from 2-3 questions each, identical to the Analogist's pattern.

### Do Clusters Separate by Sub-Mode?

**Short answer: Partially, and the pattern is topic-dependent.**

On topics where all three sub-modes are active, clusters tend to partially align with modes:

**career-change:**
- Cluster 1 "Transferable Expertise & Hybrid Pathways" — 1 Recombination, 1 Isomorphism, 1 Isomorphism (mixed, but isomorphism-heavy)
- Cluster 2 "Network Effects & Hidden Leverage Points" — 1 Recombination, 1 Recombination (pure recombination)
- Cluster 3 "Constraint Re-framing" — 1 Import, 1 Isomorphism, 1 Import (mixed, import-heavy)

**school-consolidation:**
- Cluster 1 "Identity and Infrastructure Connections" — 1 Recombination, 1 Recombination, 1 Recombination (pure recombination)
- Cluster 2 "Governance and Economic Structure Parallels" — 1 Import, 1 Recombination, 1 Isomorphism (fully mixed)
- Cluster 3 "Temporal and Design Pattern Connections" — 1 Import, 1 Isomorphism (mixed)

**wearable-device:**
- Cluster 1 "Cross-Domain Architecture Patterns" — 1 Isomorphism, 1 Import, 1 Import (import-heavy)
- Cluster 2 "Hidden Structural Relationships" — 1 Isomorphism, 1 Isomorphism (pure isomorphism)
- Cluster 3 "System Integration Opportunities" — 1 Recombination, 1 Recombination (pure recombination)

**The wearable-device topic shows the cleanest cluster-mode alignment.** One cluster is import, one is isomorphism, one is recombination. This is the ideal scenario for round-robin selection — picking one question per cluster guarantees mode diversity.

However, this clean alignment is the exception rather than the rule. Most topics show clusters organized by thematic content (not cognitive mode), with modes distributed across clusters. The cluster titles confirm this: clusters are named for topical themes ("Regulatory Parallels," "Trust Architecture," "Ecosystem Design") rather than cognitive operations.

**Space-party and tool-library have no mode separation because import dominates.** All clusters contain import questions because that is the only mode present.

### Round-Robin Implications

**At LOW effort (3 questions, one per cluster):**
- On topics with clean cluster-mode alignment (wearable-device), round-robin would capture one import, one isomorphism, one recombination question — excellent mode diversity.
- On most topics, round-robin would capture thematic diversity (the clusters organize by topic, not mode) but mode diversity would be luck-dependent. However, since most clusters contain a mix of modes, the round-robin would likely capture at least 2 of 3 modes.
- On space-party, round-robin captures 3 import questions because no other modes exist. The Connector provides no distinctive value over the Analogist.

**At MEDIUM effort (5 questions):**
- More robust mode capture. On balanced topics, 5 questions from 3 clusters would almost certainly include all three modes.
- On space-party and tool-library, still import-dominated.

**At HIGH effort (all questions):**
- Full output captured. Mode distribution matches the per-topic percentages above.

**Conclusion:** Round-robin does not reliably separate sub-modes. The Connector's distinctive value under round-robin selection is topic-dependent, not architecturally guaranteed.

## Head-to-Head: Per-Topic Comparison

### career-change

**Connector strengths:** Excels at identifying hybrid career paths (medical-legal partnerships, medical evidence in immigration cases). Q4 ("The translator's advantage") and Q5 ("feedback loops that inform your legal education") are genuinely distinctive — they propose creating something new at the intersection of nursing and law, rather than just using nursing as a metaphor for law. The isomorphism questions (Q2, Q3, Q7) go beyond "nursing is like law" to identify that the same structural problems (system navigation, burnout sources, high-stakes decision-making) manifest in both fields. This produces more actionable insight for the career-change decision.

**Analogist strengths:** More vivid and varied metaphors. "Technical debt" (Q2), "phenotypic plasticity" (Q5), "circuit rider" (Q6), and "Hollywood extra to series regular" (Q7) each draw from genuinely different source domains and create memorable reframings. The Analogist's questions are more diverse in their imagery, even if each individual question stays within the "import a metaphor" mode.

**Winner for Perspective slot: Connector.** This is a multi-domain reconciliation topic (healthcare + law + personal finance + identity). The Connector's isomorphism and recombination modes produce questions that are more directly useful for the actual career decision — they help the user see how their two worlds connect structurally, rather than offering metaphorical lenses from unrelated domains. The Connector's Q1-Q5 represent territory the Analogist cannot reach.

### food-truck

**Connector strengths:** Q4 (regulatory constraint as market opportunity) and Q5 (food truck ecosystem as symbiotic network) show genuine isomorphism thinking — reframing the competitive landscape as a structural problem with parallels to ecology and urban planning. Q3 proposes combining food truck with existing brick-and-mortar venues as a partnership strategy.

The Analogist also covers hub-and-spoke logistics (Q6), technology platform network effects (Q7), and regulatory collective action (Q8), spanning more source domains overall.

**Winner for Perspective slot: Analogist.** The food truck topic is fundamentally a single-domain business planning challenge. The Analogist's rich metaphorical vocabulary produces more diverse reframings. The Connector's import questions (Q1, Q2, Q6 — MVP, SaaS, franchise) are less vivid and more conventional than the Analogist's ecological metaphors. The Connector's distinctive modes (Q4, Q5) add value but don't compensate for the overall metaphorical quality gap.

## Swap Criteria Recommendation

### When to Swap Analogist -> Connector

The data reveals that multi-domain complexity alone is not a reliable swap signal. The youth-mentorship and mobile-app topics were predicted as LOW complexity for the Connector, yet the Connector outperforms the Analogist on both. Meanwhile, food-truck (predicted MODERATE) favors the Analogist.

The more reliable signal is the **nature of the inter-domain relationship** in the topic:

**Swap when the topic requires reconciling systems that interact with each other:**
- Career-change: Two career systems (nursing, law) that must be bridged by a single person
- Mobile-app: Multiple stakeholder systems (users, team, investors, codebase) whose problems are structurally linked
- School-consolidation: Multiple institutional systems (districts, unions, communities, state) that must be merged
- Wearable-device: Multiple technical systems (hardware, ML, privacy, regulatory) whose constraints interact
- Youth-mentorship: Multiple social systems (faith organization, schools, juvenile justice, volunteers) whose referral and safety structures must interoperate

**The common thread is not "multiple domains exist" but "the domains constrain each other."** When the topic's challenge is fundamentally about how different systems interact, the Connector's isomorphism and recombination modes activate because there are genuine structural relationships to identify and genuine combinations to propose.

**Specific swap criteria for the orchestrator:**

1. The topic explicitly involves **bridging, merging, or reconciling** distinct operational domains (not just drawing inspiration from them)
2. The topic's key challenges arise from **interactions between domains** rather than within a single domain
3. The user's question implies a need to **create something new at the intersection** of multiple fields, not just understand one field better through metaphor

### When to Keep Analogist

**Keep the Analogist when the topic is fundamentally about understanding one domain better through metaphorical reframing:**
- Space-party: A single event planning challenge that benefits from imported design principles
- Tool-library: A single community initiative that benefits from borrowed models from other sharing systems
- Food-truck: A single business that benefits from ecological and strategic metaphors

**The common thread is that the topic has a clear primary domain** and the value of the Perspective persona is to illuminate that domain from outside angles, not to reconcile multiple domains.

**Specific keep-Analogist signals:**
1. The topic has a single clear "home domain" — one field/industry/context where the work lives
2. The challenge is about doing something better within that domain, not about bridging domains
3. The user's question is "how should I think about X?" not "how do I connect X and Y?"

### Ambiguous Cases

**habit-tracker and property-management** are genuinely ambiguous:

- **Habit-tracker:** Could be framed as single-domain (web app design) or multi-domain (behavioral science + technology + community design). The Connector produces balanced sub-mode output, suggesting both framings are valid. Default: Analogist (the app is the primary domain), unless the user's framing emphasizes behavioral science integration.

- **Property-management:** Multi-domain on paper (hospitality + real estate + regulation + community) but the domains are all facets of one business operation rather than genuinely distinct systems. The Connector and Analogist perform comparably. Default: Analogist, unless the user emphasizes the regulatory/community tension as a multi-system reconciliation challenge.

- **food-truck:** Despite moderate multi-domain complexity, the Analogist wins because the domains (culinary, business, regulation) are all subordinate to a single entrepreneurial challenge. The Connector's import mode produces less vivid analogies than the Analogist on this type of topic. Default: Analogist.

**General ambiguity rule:** When in doubt, keep the Analogist. The Analogist is a CONFIRMED Tier 1 persona with excellent cross-topic consistency. The Connector adds distinctive value only when its non-import modes activate, which requires genuine inter-domain reconciliation in the topic.

## Append Strategy Assessment

### Self-Contained Questions

The Connector's questions are fully self-contained. Each question names its source domain or structural parallel, develops the connection, and poses a specific question. No question depends on another for context. This matches the Analogist's append suitability.

However, the Connector's questions are systematically **longer** than the Analogist's — typically 3-4 sentences vs. the Analogist's 2-3 sentences. Each Connector question includes more internal development of the structural parallel. This is not a problem for append treatment, but it does mean the Connector's appended output adds more tokens to Phase 2C than the Analogist's equivalent.

### Predictable Cluster Structure

The Connector matches the Analogist's perfect cluster consistency: exactly 3 clusters per topic, 2-3 questions per cluster. This makes round-robin selection equally straightforward.

### Voice Preservation Under Round-Robin

The Connector's voice is distinctive but **mode-dependent:**

- **When isomorphism/recombination modes are active:** The voice is clearly different from the Analogist. Questions contain phrases like "structurally identical," "the same problem," "what if we combined," "how might we position [topic] at the intersection." A single selected question would signal "this is Connector territory, not Analogist territory."

- **When import mode dominates:** The voice is difficult to distinguish from the Analogist. Both personas say "what if we borrowed from X" or "how might principles from Y inform Z." The Connector's import-mode questions are functionally identical to Analogist questions in tone and structure.

**Implication:** On topics where the Connector adds distinctive value (career-change, school-consolidation, wearable-device, youth-mentorship, mobile-app), round-robin selection at any effort level would preserve its distinctive voice because at least one cluster will contain isomorphism or recombination questions. On topics where the Connector falls back to import mode (space-party, tool-library), voice is indistinguishable from the Analogist.

### Would 3 Selected Questions (LOW Effort) Capture Distinctive Value?

**On balanced/distinctive topics:** Yes, with high probability. At 25-38% import rate, round-robin across 3 clusters would capture at least 1-2 distinctive-mode questions. On wearable-device (with clean cluster-mode alignment), round-robin would reliably capture one of each mode.

**On import-dominant topics:** No. At 63-100% import rate, round-robin captures import questions only. The Connector provides zero distinctive value at low effort on these topics.

### Overall Append Assessment

The Connector inherits the Analogist's append mechanics cleanly: same cluster count, same cluster sizes, same self-contained question structure, same round-robin compatibility. The functional difference is that the Connector's append value is topic-dependent (tied to sub-mode activation), while the Analogist's append value is topic-independent (consistently strong metaphorical reframing regardless of topic).

## Summary Recommendation

### Swap Decision

The Connector is a legitimate Analogist replacement **on specific topic types** but should not be treated as a universal upgrade. The data supports a conditional swap:

**Use the Connector when:**
- The topic requires bridging, merging, or reconciling distinct systems that constrain each other
- The user's question is about connecting domains, not just understanding one domain better
- Examples: career transitions across fields, multi-system institutional change, cross-disciplinary technical design, social program design spanning multiple institutional systems

**Keep the Analogist when:**
- The topic has a clear single primary domain
- The value of the Perspective persona is creative metaphorical reframing
- The topic is concrete, bounded, or operational
- Examples: event planning, single-business strategy, single-product design, single-community initiatives

**Default: Analogist.** The Analogist is the safer choice across all topic types. The Connector adds distinctive value on approximately 5 of 10 topics (50%), but on the remaining topics it either matches the Analogist (ties) or underperforms (import-mode collapse). The Analogist never has a catastrophic mode collapse — it performs consistently well across all topics.

### Volume Range

The Connector performs well at 5-8 questions. No evidence suggests a different range would be better. The 7-8 question tendency (vs. the Analogist's 6-8) is not concerning.

### Append Confirmation

The Connector inherits the Analogist's append treatment cleanly. Same cluster structure (exactly 3), same self-containment, same round-robin compatibility. No modifications to the Perspective persona append protocol are needed.

### Caveats

1. **The space-party result (100% import) is a hard constraint.** On simple, concrete, single-domain topics, the Connector provides zero additional value over the Analogist. The orchestrator must avoid swapping on these topics.

2. **The youth-mentorship result complicates the "multi-domain complexity" heuristic.** This topic was predicted as LOW complexity but the Connector outperforms the Analogist. The better heuristic is "inter-system reconciliation," not "domain count."

3. **Only one test run per topic was conducted.** The sub-mode distributions reported here could shift by 1-2 questions in either direction on a rerun. The broad patterns (space-party = all import, school-consolidation = recombination-heavy) are likely stable, but the exact percentages should be treated as estimates.

4. **The Connector's encoding artifact (Chinese character in youth-mentorship Q1) suggests monitoring for prompt/model artifacts** if the Connector is deployed in production.
