## Quality Assessment

**At q08-12:** Quality is consistently high across all topics. Questions are well-bounded, each targeting a distinct stakeholder concern. The persona reliably identifies underserved populations relevant to each topic:
- Career-change: immigrant patients, refugees, communities who define "help" differently
- Food-truck: low-income customers, people with disabilities, Filipino/Mexican communities (cultural authenticity)
- Habit-tracker: neurodivergent users, those with unstable routines, people with low digital literacy
- School-consolidation: students with long bus rides, families without transportation, special education students
- Youth-mentorship: justice-involved teens, families skeptical of institutions, teens with scheduling barriers

This confirms V1 take 2's finding that at 5-10 questions, each focuses on "one stakeholder dimension" — the same holds at 8-12. Cluster counts are appropriate (3-5) and questions within clusters are non-redundant.

**At q10-15:** Quality remains strong. The additional questions explore secondary stakeholder dimensions without significant degradation:
- Property-management at 13 questions covers guests, neighbors, property owners, workers, and regulatory stakeholders — each cluster addresses a genuinely distinct audience
- School-consolidation at 15 questions separates student experience, family impact, and equity/access into well-bounded clusters
- Wearable-device at 13 questions covers trust/transparency, accessibility/inclusion, privacy/autonomy, and daily life/emotional experience — all distinct

The V1 take 2 characterization of questions at this range as "properly bounded" and "excellent coverage of the user advocacy landscape" is confirmed.

## Filler Assessment

**V1 take 2 prediction:** Filler appears at ~13 questions, with ~10-15% filler at 15-20 volume.

**Observed at q10-15:** Filler is minimal and aligns with the V1 take 2 estimate. At runs that hit 14-15 questions, marginal filler appears in these forms:
- **Overlap between adjacent concerns:** In the career-change q10-15 run, questions about "burnout vs. calling" (#6) and "sustainable advocacy" (#14) cover overlapping emotional territory. In the food-truck q10-15 run, questions about cultural authenticity (#1) and trust-building through transparency (#13-14) share thematic space.
- **Broad framing of narrow concerns:** Some questions at the tail end of longer runs use expansive framing that could be consolidated (e.g., the food-truck q10-15 question #12 about "accountability to the community beyond market success" covers similar ground to #10 about "community voice and agency").

These patterns are consistent with the V1 take 2 finding that filler at this level is around 10-15% and takes the form of "narrow sub-populations or edge cases that could be consolidated." The filler threshold of ~13 questions appears accurate — runs at 13 or fewer show no meaningful redundancy, while those at 14-15 show 1-2 questions that overlap with existing coverage.

**Notably, filler remains lower than typical perspective personas.** This confirms V1 take 2's observation that "there are genuinely many distinct stakeholder concerns to explore."

## Cross-Topic Consistency

**V1 take 2 prediction:** "Very consistent. The Audience Advocate identifies underserved populations relevant to each topic with equal facility."

**Confirmed.** The persona demonstrates remarkable adaptability across wildly different topic domains:

- **Technical topics** (wearable-device, habit-tracker, mobile-app): The persona successfully translates its advocacy lens to technology contexts — sensor bias across skin tones, digital literacy barriers, accessibility for neurodivergent users, domestic privacy concerns with wearables. No awkward shoehorning of equity concerns.
- **Community/social topics** (school-consolidation, youth-mentorship, tool-library): Natural fit. These topics have obvious stakeholder diversity, and the persona excels at surfacing hidden costs and power dynamics.
- **Business topics** (food-truck, property-management): The persona bridges between customer experience, worker treatment, and community impact without losing business relevance.
- **Personal decision topics** (career-change): The persona reframes the individual decision through the lens of community need and positionality — who would be served, whose voices matter in the decision.
- **Lightest topic** (space-party): Even here, the persona generates substantive questions about sensory accessibility, socioeconomic inclusion, neurodivergent children, and cultural assumptions — demonstrating that the advocacy lens scales down gracefully to low-stakes topics.

No topic type exposed a weakness. The seven new topics (career-change, food-truck, mobile-app, property-management, school-consolidation, wearable-device, youth-mentorship) are fully consistent with the original three (space-party, tool-library, habit-tracker).

## Category Confirmation

**V1 take 2 assigned:** Structural (alongside Systems Thinker)

**Confirmed.** The Audience Advocate behaves as a Structural persona across all evidence:

1. **Consistent framework application:** Every topic gets the same structural treatment — identify stakeholders, surface hidden barriers, examine power dynamics, center marginalized perspectives. The framework is stable across topics.
2. **Volume scales with stakeholder complexity, not topic novelty:** The persona generates more or fewer questions based on how many distinct stakeholder groups exist, not based on how "interesting" the topic is. This is characteristic of Structural personas whose output volume correlates with structural dimensions of the problem.
3. **Low filler at high volumes:** Structural personas maintain quality at higher volumes because their framework (stakeholder analysis) naturally generates distinct concerns. The Audience Advocate's ~10-15% filler at 15 questions, compared to higher filler rates for pure perspective personas, is consistent with Structural classification.
4. **Cluster structure reflects stakeholder groups, not arbitrary themes:** Clusters map to audience segments (guests, neighbors, workers, owners in property-management; students, families, communities in school-consolidation) rather than abstract categories.

## Notable Deviations

1. **Wearable-device overshoots at q08-12.** At 15 questions in the 8-12 bucket, this is the only meaningful compliance failure. The technical health device with its many stakeholder dimensions (patients, caregivers, people with disabilities, domestic violence survivors, diverse physiologies, digitally inexperienced users) apparently exceeded the persona's usual self-regulation. This suggests that **highly technical topics with obvious equity implications may push the Audience Advocate above its target range** at the lower bucket. This could be addressed with stronger volume constraints in the prompt, but it is a minor concern — the quality of all 15 questions is high.

2. **Ceiling tendency at q10-15.** 7/10 runs produce 14-15 questions. The persona consistently maximizes within its range rather than centering. This is not a problem (all are within range), but it suggests the 10-15 bucket could potentially be tightened to 12-15 for the Audience Advocate specifically if context window economy were a concern.

3. **Cluster count is more variable than expected.** q10-15 runs range from 3 to 5 clusters. The lower cluster counts (3) appear on topics where stakeholder groups map cleanly to a few broad categories (career-change: community reality, career transition, resources/systems; school-consolidation: students, families, equity). Higher cluster counts (5) appear when stakeholder groups fragment (habit-tracker: user experience, accessibility, trust, dignity, edge cases). This variability is natural and does not indicate inconsistency — it reflects genuine structural differences in stakeholder landscapes.

4. **Quality may be slightly *better* than V1 take 2 described at higher volumes.** V1 take 2 noted "minor filler" at 15-20 questions. At 14-15 questions (our observed ceiling), filler is genuinely minimal. The stabilized 10-15 bucket may represent the true sweet spot where this persona delivers maximum coverage with negligible waste.
