#### Critical Finding: The Nature of "Progressive Elaboration"

The Momentum Builder's signature move is embedding multi-step chains within single questions: "Starting with X... what if Y... and then imagine Z." This is a distinctive cognitive pattern, but it is **convergent elaboration dressed in generative language**. Here is the key evidence:

The typical Momentum Builder question structure is:
1. **Generative seed** — a genuinely new concept or reframe (valuable)
2. **Progressive elaboration** — "starting from there, we could..." (convergent — narrows toward specific implementation)
3. **Aspirational extension** — "and if that works, imagine..." (pseudo-generative — extends the same idea rather than opening new territory)

This means many questions that *feel* generative are actually **one generative idea surrounded by convergent scaffolding**. The generative seed is typically 1-2 sentences; the convergent elaboration is 3-6 sentences. The signal-to-noise ratio for Phase 2B purposes is low.

#### Unique Momentum Builder Territory (Questions the Pragmatist Would Never Ask)

These questions are distinctively Momentum Builder and have no Pragmatist equivalent:

1. "What if the birthday girl's excitement became the engine that powers the whole experience?" — *Uses emotional energy as design input*
2. "From birthday party to family tradition — what's the seed?" — *Temporal expansion beyond the event*

4. "What if breaks in routine were treated as 'momentum tests' — challenges that, when passed, make your habit practice stronger?" — *Anti-fragility reframe*

7. "What if habit completion created visible, shareable momentum artifacts?" — *Novel artifact/gamification concept*

These questions are genuinely generative and represent territory that no convergent persona would cover. However, they are the **generative seeds** within longer, more elaborate question blocks — the surrounding progressive elaboration adds convergent scaffolding.

#### Progressive Elaboration Pattern Analysis

The Momentum Builder's signature is the multi-step chain. Here is a representative example:

> "Starting with simple 'you're not alone' indicators showing how many others are working on similar habits today, **we could build toward** optional pairing with a 'momentum buddy' for mutual encouragement. **And if that works, imagine taking it further:** what if small teams could form around shared goals, creating collective momentum where your progress contributes to something larger than yourself?"

This structure appears in **every single Momentum Builder question across all three topics**. The pattern is:
- Seed idea (1-2 sentences)
- "Starting from there / Starting with..." (implementation first step)
- "we could build toward..." (implementation second step)
- "And if that works, imagine..." (aspirational extension)
- Sometimes a fourth layer: "And once that's working, what if..."

The Pragmatist never uses this pattern. Pragmatist questions are **single-layer**: one question, one concern, sometimes with explanatory context but never with progressive chains.

**Implication for Phase 2B:** The progressive elaboration pattern is problematic for question generation because it:
1. Embeds answers within questions (pre-narrows the brainstorming space)
2. Creates long question blocks that are harder for Phase 2C synthesis to process
3. Blurs the line between "question" and "proposal" — many MB questions are closer to mini-proposals than open questions

### Recommendation

**For Phase 2B roster: Exclude (keep for Phase 3)**

Rationale:

1. **Mixed generative/convergent profile (59/41%)** — Not generative enough for a question generation role. Compare with the Questioner or Analyst, which are expected to be more purely generative in their questioning.

2. **Progressive elaboration embeds answers in questions** — The MB's signature pattern of "starting with X, building toward Y, imagining Z" pre-narrows the brainstorming space. Phase 2B questions should open territory, not propose implementation paths. The generative seeds are valuable, but they are buried in convergent scaffolding.

3. **Topic sensitivity creates inconsistency** — The 45%-73% generative range across topics means the MB would be strongly generative for some sessions and majority convergent for others. Fixed Phase 2B assignments need consistent performers.

4. **Low overlap with Pragmatist confirms distinctiveness but not Phase 2B fit** — The MB is clearly distinct from the Pragmatist (only 9.8% convergence), but being "not the Pragmatist" is not sufficient for Phase 2B inclusion. The question is whether the MB's territory is better served in Phase 2B (question generation) or Phase 3 (brainstorming), and the evidence points to Phase 3.

5. **High selflessness is a Phase 2B positive but insufficient alone** — The MB's questions are broadly answerable, which is good for question generation. However, the progressive elaboration format works against Phase 2B's purpose of generating clean, open questions.

6. **Phase 3 is the natural home** — The MB's progressive elaboration pattern is precisely what Phase 3 brainstorming needs: taking ideas and building them into action-oriented sequences. The "starting with... building toward... imagining..." structure is an elaboration engine, which is Phase 3's core task.

**If there is a strong desire to add implementation-oriented questions to Phase 2B**, the MB's generative seeds (without the elaboration scaffolding) would be valuable. However, stripping the elaboration would require either (a) prompt engineering to constrain the MB's output format, or (b) post-processing to extract seeds from chains. Neither is practical for the current architecture.

### Notes

1. **The "answer-in-question" problem is the key finding.** The Momentum Builder does not simply ask questions — it proposes implementation paths framed as questions. This is the core reason it belongs in Phase 3 (where elaboration is the goal) rather than Phase 2B (where open questioning is the goal). Example: "What if we started with one simple, guaranteed-to-succeed craft, celebrated the results together, and then used those creations as either party decorations or take-home gifts" is a proposal, not a question.

2. **The generative seeds are high quality.** Stripped of elaboration, concepts like "streak system celebrating recovery," "momentum transmission between users," "constraints as creativity source," and "birthday party to family tradition" are genuinely creative reframes. These seeds rival what a Visionary or Creative Director might produce. The problem is packaging, not ideation quality.

3. **The habit tracker topic reveals the MB's ceiling.** At 73% generative with genuinely novel feature concepts (anti-fragility, momentum seasons, identity transformation through habits), the habit tracker output shows what the MB can do at its best. But this is the best case — for operational topics, the ratio drops to 45%.

4. **Question count consistency is interesting.** The MB produced 16-17 questions for space party and tool library, and 15 for habit tracker — yet the habit tracker file was 11.5 KB versus 6.9-7.9 KB for others. This confirms the variance is in elaboration depth, not question count. The habit tracker questions had 3-4 layers of progressive elaboration each, versus 2-3 layers for other topics.

5. **Connection to I16 (Analyst vs DA head-to-head):** If the Analyst is confirmed as a strong Phase 2B performer, the MB's strategic/systems questions would be redundant there. The Analyst likely covers the analytical dimension that the MB barely touches (2.0%), while the MB's human-centered and creative dimensions are better served by the Audience Advocate and existing roster personas.

6. **The "progressive elaboration" cognitive mode is definitively convergent for Phase 2B purposes.** It generates a path from A to B to C — this is convergence (narrowing toward a specific trajectory) even when each step is creative. True generative questioning would ask "What are all the possible directions from A?" rather than "If A, then B, then C." The MB answers its own question before the brainstormers can.
