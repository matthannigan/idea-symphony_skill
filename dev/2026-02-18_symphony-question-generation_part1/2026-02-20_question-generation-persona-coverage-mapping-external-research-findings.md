# Question Generation Persona Coverage Mapping — External Research Findings

**Date:** 2026-02-20
**Purpose:** Map Idea Symphony personas against question generation methodologies to assess coverage, identify gaps, and evaluate the current and potential Phase 2B persona set.

***

## 1. Executive Summary

Idea Symphony’s 23 personas collectively cover a broad range of analytical, generative, human-centered, and systems-oriented question modes, but there is a strong native bias toward analytical, evaluative, and risk-focused questioning.  Generative, appreciative, narrative, and meta-process question types are covered by a small subset of personas and are therefore structurally fragile if those personas are omitted from Phase 2B.

Mapping against the unified taxonomy of question types (clarification, assumption-probing, evidence-seeking, viewpoint-challenging, implication, divergent exploratory, convergent focusing, generative what-if/HMW, evaluative risk/feasibility, appreciative/strengths, narrative/experiential, systems/second-order, emotional/values, meta-process) shows strong coverage in assumption-probing, evaluative risk, and convergent focusing, with weaker coverage in appreciative, metaprocess, and pure recall/comprehension questions.  Question quality dimensions (open-endedness, constraint balance, specificity, assumption challenge, invitation to imagination, positive framing) are best served when analytical personas are combined with generative, appreciative, and human-centered personas; analytical-only rosters underweight imagination and positive framing.

For Phase 2B, the most intrinsically powerful question generators—balancing volume, diversity of dimensions, distinctiveness of cluster structure, and “selfless” territory-opening—are: The Questioner, The Analyst, The Devil’s Advocate, The Storyteller, The Analogist, The Appreciative Inquirer, The Provocateur, The Visionary, and (with caveats) The Momentum Builder.  Human-centered (Audience Advocate, Empath), systems/strategic (Systems Thinker, Futurist), and reframing/economic/legal lenses (Constraint Flipper, Accountant, Lawyer, Politician, Pragmatist, Technical Expert, Simplifier, Connector, Synthesizer) add important coverage but are generally better as answerers or situational question generators rather than core Phase 2B workhorses.

Given the isolated-subagent architecture, the most critical cognitive roles for Phase 2B question generation are:

- **Meta-inquiry and assumption-challenging** (Questioner, Devil’s Advocate, First Principles Thinker) to prevent narrow framing.
- **Strong divergent/generative disruptors** (Provocateur, Visionary, Analogist, Constraint Flipper, Momentum Builder) to counteract analytical fixation and open non-obvious territories.
- **Affirmative and human-centered framers** (Appreciative Inquirer, Storyteller, Audience Advocate, Empath) to add strengths-based, experiential, and equity-empathy perspectives that analytical personas systematically miss.
- **Systems/second-order thinkers** (Systems Thinker, Futurist) to cover long-term consequences and ecosystem dynamics.

### 2.2 Coverage Summary

**Taxonomy coverage.** Across all 23 personas, the system is very strong in:

- **Assumption-probing and evidence-seeking:** Analyst, Questioner, Devil’s Advocate, First Principles Thinker, Accountant, Lawyer, Pragmatist, Technical Expert all foreground structural decomposition, evidence demands, and risk/feasibility assessments.
- **Evaluative risk and convergent focusing:** Devil’s Advocate, Lawyer, Pragmatist, Simplifier, Futurist, Systems Thinker, Accountant strongly weight evaluative and convergent questions about what could go wrong, trade-offs, and prioritization.

Coverage is moderate in:

- **Generative what-if/HMW and divergent exploratory questions:** Provided primarily by Visionary, Provocateur, Analogist, Momentum Builder, Constraint Flipper, Appreciative Inquirer, and to a lesser extent Questioner and Storyteller.
- **Systems/second-order and implication questions:** Systems Thinker, Futurist, Politician, First Principles Thinker, Devil’s Advocate, and Momentum Builder cover downstream effects and feedback loops, but only a few personas specialize in this.

Coverage is relatively weak or fragile in:

- **Appreciative/strengths-based questions:** Almost entirely concentrated in Appreciative Inquirer, with small contributions from Momentum Builder and Visionary; removing AI removes most strengths-based territory.
- **Narrative/experiential questions:** Concentrated in Storyteller and, secondarily, Audience Advocate and Empath; without Storyteller, narrative coverage drops sharply.
- **Metaprocess reflection and “question of questions”:** Synthesizer and Questioner contribute here, but there is no dedicated process-manager persona in Phase 2B by design; metacognitive coverage is present but relatively light.
- **Recall/knowledge/comprehension questions:** Bloom’s lower tiers are scarcely represented; almost all personas assume a conceptual baseline and move directly to analysis, synthesis, or evaluation.

**Quality dimensions.** Using the question quality rubric (open-endedness, constraint, specificity, assumptions, analytic vs imaginative invitation, positive framing):

- **Open-endedness:** Generative personas (Visionary, Provocateur, Analogist, Appreciative Inquirer, Storyteller, Questioner) excel, while Accountant, Lawyer, Technical Expert, Pragmatist, Simplifier often pose narrower, more evaluative questions.
- **Constraint balance and specificity:** Analytical and specialist personas provide highly constrained and specific questions, which is valuable for implementation but can limit divergent exploration if over-represented.
- **Assumption freedom:** Questioner, Devil’s Advocate, First Principles Thinker, Constraint Flipper, and Provocateur are strong here, consistently surfacing and attacking hidden premises.
- **Invitation to imagination:** Visionary, Provocateur, Analogist, Appreciative Inquirer, Momentum Builder, Storyteller invite imaginative responses; Analyst, Accountant, Lawyer, Pragmatist, Technical Expert rarely do.
- **Positive framing:** Appreciative Inquirer and Visionary lean strongly positive; Devil’s Advocate, Lawyer, and other risk personas lean negative; most analytical personas are neutral.

The external research warns that analytical-heavy question sets exacerbate LLM fixation bias and narrow the “question-response loop,” making downstream brainstorming more conservative and consensus-bound.  In an isolated-subagent architecture, the only way to simulate high “collective intelligence” is to explicitly engineer diversity across generative, analytical, human-centered, and systems modes at question time.

### 2.3 Question-Generation Fitness Rankings

Ordering here is from **most likely to be used** in Phase 2B (high intrinsic fitness and coverage value) to **least likely** (primarily answerers or very situational question generators).

**Tier 1 – Core question generators (default Phase 2B backbone):**

- Questioner, Analyst, Devil’s Advocate, Storyteller, Analogist, Appreciative Inquirer, Provocateur, Visionary.

**Tier 2 – Strong but situational or mixed-mode generators:**

- Momentum Builder, Audience Advocate, Systems Thinker, First Principles Thinker, Constraint Flipper, Empath, Futurist, Synthesizer (mainly Phase 4).

**Tier 3 – Primarily answerers / topic-gated generators:**

- Accountant, Lawyer, Politician, Pragmatist, Technical Expert, Simplifier, Connector.

Within Tier 1 and Tier 2, ordering in the next section reflects both distinctiveness of question territory and empirical findings about selflessness, volume, and cluster diversity.

## 3. Detailed Persona Analyses

Personas are ordered from most likely to be deployed for Phase 2B question generation to least, assuming a general-purpose brainstorming context. In all cases, suitability is evaluated under the isolated-subagent constraint: each persona must produce high-value questions independently, without facilitation or dialogue with other agents.

### 3.1 The Questioner

#### 3.1.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Extremely well-suited; question generation is this persona’s native function.
- Ideal as a Phase 2B anchor persona, especially at medium-effort settings where only 3–5 personas can be run.


#### 3.1.2 Evidence

- Produces the highest average number of distinct questions per topic (~24), exceeding other analytical personas by ~40%.
- Question portfolio is multi-dimensional: ~49% analytical, 39% human-centered, 28% provocative, 21% creative, with strong equity and inclusion territory that was not predicted by the persona description.
- Quantitative analysis shows very low convergence (~13%) with First Principles Thinker despite both being labeled Analytical, confirming distinctive territory.


#### 3.1.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use in nearly all Phase 2B rosters as a meta-question generator to widen problem framing before more specialized personas fire.
- Pair with Devil’s Advocate and First Principles Thinker to ensure both creative and structural assumptions are surfaced; and with Storyteller or Audience Advocate to turn analytical probes into lived-experience scenarios.


#### 3.1.4 Notes

- Over-generates relative to the nominal 15–20 question target, but this additional breadth is valuable for synthesis and can be managed via prompt tuning or downstream clustering.
- High “selflessness”: many questions explicitly open territory for other personas (e.g., Empath, Systems Thinker, Constraint Flipper).

***

### 3.2 The Analyst

#### 3.2.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Highly suitable; provides systematic coverage and clear, well-structured cluster scaffolding ideal for synthesis.
- Should generally be included whenever analytical coverage is desired, especially if Devil’s Advocate is also present.


#### 3.2.2 Evidence

- Generates consistent 5-cluster outputs with moderate volume (~7 KB), low variance across topics (CV ~9.7), and balanced cluster sizes.
- Dimensional profile is strongly analytical (~71%) with strategic and mild systems coverage; produces zero provocative questions across topics, making it a clean constructive-analytical persona.
- Investigation with Devil’s Advocate shows no direct cluster overlap; each persona covers different aspects (framework optimization vs. stress-testing).


#### 3.2.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include as a core analytic persona in Phase 2B to ensure the problem space is decomposed into coherent, evaluable components.
- Combine with Devil’s Advocate and a generative persona (Visionary or Analogist) to approximate a full CPS Clarify–Ideate–Develop coverage within an isolated architecture.


#### 3.2.4 Notes

- Question sets are highly selfless: any Phase 3 persona can answer them, and they rarely pre-prescribe solutions.
- Risk of analytical bias arises only if Analyst is paired exclusively with other analytical/evaluative personas with no generative counterweights.

***

### 3.3 The Devil’s Advocate

#### 3.3.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Highly suited; uniquely covers failure modes, equity/risk concerns, and unintended consequences not surfaced by other analytical personas.
- Recommended as a core member of any analytically robust Phase 2B roster.


#### 3.3.2 Evidence

- Dimensional profile shows ~53% provocative, with strong human-centered and tactical subdimensions; only ~2% analytical.
- Opens unique clusters such as Privacy and Trust Concerns, Psychological Harm, and Competitive Blind Spots that the Analyst never generates, especially on complex topics (habit tracker).
- Topic sensitivity is adaptive: output volume expands on complex topics with many “attack surfaces” and contracts on narrow topics, indicating intelligent allocation of questioning effort.


#### 3.3.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use in all but the most lighthearted sessions (where heavy risk framing might be undesirable); particularly critical for products, data, and civic infrastructure topics.
- Pair with Appreciative Inquirer or Visionary to maintain balance between risk discovery and aspirational exploration, countering framing bias.


#### 3.3.4 Notes

- Functions as Six Hats Black Hat in an architecture without live facilitation; must be deliberately balanced by Yellow/Green (Visionary, Appreciative, Provocateur) personas to avoid pessimistic anchoring.

***

### 3.4 The Storyteller

#### 3.4.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Excellent; provides narrative/experiential question territory largely missing from analytical personas, making it a top divergent candidate.


#### 3.4.2 Evidence

- Produces narrative-arc clusters (e.g., Arrival, First Impressions; Hero’s Journey) that no analytical persona approximates.
- Investigation of distinctive cluster framers finds Storyteller to be one of the six personas with genuinely unique cluster structures, and the strongest at opening territory for other personas.
- Questions are notably selfless: they invite Empath, Visionary, Pragmatist, and Audience Advocate to step into scenarios rather than presupposing Storyteller’s own answers.


#### 3.4.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include Storyteller whenever user experience, engagement, or emotional journey matters, or when countering an overly analytical question set.
- Pair with Audience Advocate and Empath for rich, multi-perspective human-centered question spaces, and with Systems Thinker to connect micro-stories to macro-patterns.


#### 3.4.4 Notes

- Narrative questions implicitly encode time, conflict, and resolution, which is beneficial for downstream brainstorming but can complicate clustering if over-elaborated; instructions should keep questions concise.

***

### 3.5 The Analogist

#### 3.5.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Highly suitable as a divergent/connective persona; particularly strong for complex or stuck problem spaces where lateral analogies are valuable.


#### 3.5.2 Evidence

- Identified as one of the six personas with distinctive cluster structures (e.g., Natural Systems and Biological Analogies, Historical and Cultural Models).
- Investigation of generative framers finds that Analogist introduces conceptual frameworks—mycorrhizal networks, keystone habits, desire lines—that no analytical persona generates.


#### 3.5.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use alongside Visionary and Provocateur to provide multiple distinct paths to divergence: reimagination (Visionary), provocation (Provocateur), and analogy (Analogist).
- For highly technical or regulated domains, pair with Technical Expert or Lawyer to ensure imported analogies respect real constraints.


#### 3.5.4 Notes

- Some questions are self-answering—Analogist is the best-equipped persona to exploit its own analogies—but the conceptual scaffolding is broadly usable by other personas.

***

### 3.6 The Appreciative Inquirer

#### 3.6.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Very high; provides the primary strengths-based, affirmative question mode in the persona library and uses a robust, named framework (AI 4-D).


#### 3.6.2 Evidence

- Empirical analysis shows consistent use of Discovery–Dream–Design–Destiny clusters across topics, with a sometimes-added Amplifying Joy cluster; clusters are fewer (4–5) but denser (5–8 questions each).
- Post-prompt-revision, strengths-grounded questions rose to ~39–48% of total, with balanced coverage across all phases; no other persona produces this pattern.
- Structurally distinctive and medium-high volume (~70 actual questions across 3 topics) with high cross-topic consistency (CV ~11.5).


#### 3.6.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include in medium and high-effort Phase 2B rosters whenever there is risk that analytical or risk personas will dominate framing.
- Pair with Devil’s Advocate and Analyst to create a dual lens of “what’s strong” and “what can break,” aligning with research that balanced positive and critical framings improve group performance.


#### 3.6.4 Notes

- Topic sensitivity leans slightly toward community and organizational contexts, where appreciative questions fit naturally; still performs adequately on technical products.

***

### 3.7 The Provocateur

#### 3.7.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Highly suitable as a lateral-disruption persona; its questions are among the most distinctive in the corpus.


#### 3.7.2 Evidence

- Dimensional profile: ~52 primary Provocative with Creative as frequent secondary dimension; nearly every question combines absurdity with a directed “real insight” suffix that translates provocation into actionable territory.
- Cluster structures (e.g., The Anti-Library, The Absence of Adults) are uniquely subversive and cannot be reproduced by analytical personas.


#### 3.7.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use when the risk of fixation is high or when the user explicitly wants non-obvious, edgy perspectives.
- Always pair with at least one strong analytical persona (Analyst, Pragmatist, Systems Thinker) and one human-centered persona to ensure provocations are grounded and ethically framed.


#### 3.7.4 Notes

- One habit-tracker file showed structural anomalies (empty clusters, mismatched counts), but qualitative content remained robust; format issues should be addressed in tooling, not by excluding the persona.

***

### 3.8 The Visionary

#### 3.8.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Highly suited as a clean generative persona; best deployed when transformative possibilities are desired.


#### 3.8.2 Evidence

- Produces consistent 5-cluster structures with moderate volume and topic-sensitive naming around paradigm shifts and agency.
- Comparative investigation with Provocateur shows Visionary operates via reconstructive aspiration rather than destructive challenge, making it a complementary generative mode.


#### 3.8.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include in Phase 2B when the task calls for reimagining categories (e.g., new products, systems redesign), paired with Pragmatist and Analyst to avoid ungrounded speculation.
- Combine with Appreciative Inquirer to connect aspirational futures to existing strengths rather than pure blue-sky.


#### 3.8.4 Notes

- High selflessness: questions focus on “what could this become?” rather than specifying how to do it, leaving broad room for others’ answers.

***

### 3.9 The Momentum Builder

#### 3.9.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Moderately high but mixed; powerful when generative seeds are extracted, but many questions embed solutions and progressive elaborations.


#### 3.9.2 Evidence

- Detailed analysis shows a consistent multi-step pattern: seed idea → implementation elaboration → aspirational extension, often within a single question.
- Overall mix is ~59% generative / 41% convergent, with broad but shallow dimension coverage across human-centered, systems, and strategic.


#### 3.9.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use selectively when the goal is to grow promising seeds into richer lines of inquiry, but avoid if the priority is clean, question-only input for synthesis.
- Consider prompt tuning to request shorter, less elaborated questions to reduce embedded answers.


#### 3.9.4 Notes

- Without live facilitation, long elaborative questions can crowd the question space and bias downstream responses; careful calibration is important.

***

### 3.10 The Audience Advocate

#### 3.10.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Strong situational persona, particularly for civic, social, and product topics where equity, access, and lived reality are central.


#### 3.10.2 Evidence

- Produces high-volume, topic-sensitive clusters around community, barriers, and inclusion; one of the higher-volume personas in question generation.
- Distinct from Empath by focusing on structural barriers and socio-economic factors rather than pure emotional tone.


#### 3.10.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include when user explicitly cares about marginalized stakeholders, accessibility, or real-world community impacts.
- Pair with Analyst (for metrics and frameworks) and Storyteller (for concrete user journeys) to turn concerns into rich question sets.


#### 3.10.4 Notes

- Overlap with Devil’s Advocate on equity questions is complementary: Advocate asks “who is excluded?” and DA asks “what harms could we inadvertently cause?”.

***

### 3.11 The Systems Thinker

#### 3.11.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Good complement but not a primary generator; best used to ensure systemic implications are not missed.


#### 3.11.2 Evidence

- High average file size (~10.3 KB) and often higher cluster counts (up to 7) suggest deep, sprawling question sets around feedback loops and archetypes.
- Provides unique system-archetype clusters that align with second-order question categories identified in the research.


#### 3.11.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include when the topic is infrastructural, policy, or ecosystem-level; pair with Visionary/Analogist and Devil’s Advocate for a full range of system futures and risks.


#### 3.11.4 Notes

- Without generative partners, Systems Thinker can skew toward diagnostic rather than imaginative questioning; it is best as part of a diverse roster.

***

### 3.12 The First Principles Thinker

#### 3.12.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Conditionally valuable; particularly strong when deep structural clarity is needed, but less selfless than Questioner and more analytically skewed.


#### 3.12.2 Evidence

- Very high analytical share (~87%) with strong systems secondary dimension (~27%); produces ontological and mechanistic questions the Questioner never generates.
- Overlaps with Questioner on about 13% of questions, but unique clusters focus on fundamental nature, minimal conditions, and mechanistic thresholds.


#### 3.12.3 Recommendations for Use

- Add when high-effort rosters allow more analytical bandwidth or when domain complexity demands deep structural modeling (e.g., data architecture, behavior change mechanics).


#### 3.12.4 Notes

- Many questions are best answered by First Principles Thinker itself, which reduces cross-roster selflessness relative to Questioner; this informs its lower priority.

***

### 3.13 The Constraint Flipper

#### 3.13.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Moderately strong; distinctive but partially overlapping with analytical trade-off thinking.


#### 3.13.2 Evidence

- Recognized as one of the six personas with distinctive cluster framings (e.g., Turning Age Into Advantage, Converting Liability Concerns into Educational Opportunities).
- Many questions can be addressed directly by analytical personas (e.g., budget reframe questions), which limits unique territory somewhat.


#### 3.13.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include as a secondary generative persona for topics where constraints are heavily felt (tight budgets, regulations, physical limitations).
- Pair with Lawyer, Accountant, and Pragmatist so reframed constraints are still realistic.


#### 3.13.4 Notes

- Conceptually close to Lateral Thinking “challenge” technique; particularly helpful against problem-framed prompts that might otherwise induce defensive analysis.

***

### 3.14 The Empath

#### 3.14.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Useful as a secondary human-centered persona, especially when Storyteller is present; less structurally distinctive on its own.


#### 3.14.2 Evidence

- Very high consistency (CV ~1.4) across topics suggests a stable emotional-question formula.
- Persona prompt emphasizes gut-feeling and emotional subtext without requiring logical justification, which translates into affective-value questions rather than structural ones.


#### 3.14.3 Recommendations for Use

- Add when emotional resonance and psychological safety are central concerns, such as wellness, education, or sensitive community work.
- Best in combination with Storyteller and Audience Advocate, where Empath’s gut signals can seed deeper narrative and equity questions.


#### 3.14.4 Notes

- Isolation architecture mitigates risk of emotional contagion dominating a group, but also prevents Empath from calibrating off others’ reactions; prompts should encourage diversity of emotional perspectives, not a single “vibe.”

***

### 3.15 The Futurist

#### 3.15.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Moderate; adds unique strategic/trend framing but is not a top-priority persona for small rosters.


#### 3.15.2 Evidence

- Investigation shows moderate selflessness: many questions can be answered by Analyst, Strategist-type personas, or Futurist itself (e.g., “how should we position for trend X?”).
- Some questions are trend-decorated versions of generic strategy questions; others open genuinely differentiated territory (e.g., ownership and governance structures under platform-cooperative trends).


#### 3.15.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include when roster size reaches 6–7 and the topic is civic, infrastructural, or technical; omit for purely personal/experiential contexts.


#### 3.15.4 Notes

- Functions partly like Six Hats Yellow/White with a time dimension; overlaps with Systems Thinker and Analyst but adds an explicit trajectory lens.

***

### 3.16 The Synthesizer

#### 3.16.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium but misaligned; primary value is in Phase 4 synthesis rather than initial question generation.


#### 3.16.2 Evidence

- Persona description emphasizes summarizing, identifying themes, and integrating multiple perspectives—the core tasks of Phase 4.
- Question-generation outputs (where present) lean heavily toward meta-process and convergent focusing rather than raw divergence.


#### 3.16.3 Recommendations for Use

- Reserve for Phase 4 or special meta-question tasks (e.g., “what are we not asking?”) rather than standard Phase 2B question lists.


#### 3.16.4 Notes

- Could be experimented with as a meta-question subagent (“question brainstorming”) to counter anchoring effects from early personas, but this is a distinct architectural role.

***

### 3.17 The Accountant

#### 3.17.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium-low; highly topic-dependent and mostly convergent.


#### 3.17.2 Evidence

- Medium volume, domain-specific clusters around budgets, cost structures, and sustainability; largest value appears in answer generation rather than question generation.


#### 3.17.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include only for topics where financial structure is central (business models, funding mechanisms, community budgeting).
- Pair with Appreciative Inquirer or Visionary so economic questions do not overconstrain innovation.


#### 3.17.4 Notes

- Exemplifies analytical-adaptive questioning described in the research; valuable but not diversity-maximizing for question generation.

***

### 3.18 The Lawyer

#### 3.18.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium-low; critical for regulated or liability-heavy topics, but strongly evaluative and problem-framed.


#### 3.18.2 Evidence

- Produces more clusters and the largest average file size of any persona, often 7–8 clusters heavy with legal framing.
- Clusters over-structure the space with numerous compliance slices, risking cognitive overload for synthesis.


#### 3.18.3 Recommendations for Use

- Add only when legal/regulatory exposure is part of the user’s explicit concern; otherwise, let Devil’s Advocate flag high-level risk.


#### 3.18.4 Notes

- Acts like an extreme Black Hat; given anchoring risk, questions should be intentionally mixed with appreciative and generative ones in synthesis to avoid defensive ideation.

***

### 3.19 The Politician

#### 3.19.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium; highly valuable but only when stakeholder politics are central.


#### 3.19.2 Evidence

- Only high-variance persona (CV ~30.3), producing double output for tool-library vs space-party, indicating strong topic sensitivity.
- Clusters around power, governance, coalition-building; territory distinct from Analyst and Devil’s Advocate.


#### 3.19.3 Recommendations for Use

- Deploy for civic, institutional, or organizational change topics; unnecessary for personal or purely technical questions.


#### 3.19.4 Notes

- In an isolated architecture, Politician cannot enact process design but can still generate governance and coalition questions that other personas can then answer.

***

### 3.20 The Pragmatist

#### 3.20.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium; primarily convergent and feasibility-focused, better suited to Phase 3.


#### 3.20.2 Evidence

- Low-volume, low-variance persona with focus on implementation, MVP, and realistic constraints; questions narrow scope rather than expanding it.


#### 3.20.3 Recommendations for Use

- Include sparingly when user explicitly wants feasibility questions in the Phase 2 output (e.g., for roadmap planning).


#### 3.20.4 Notes

- Provides a counterweight to Visionary and Provocateur, but if placed too early or overrepresented it may prematurely converge the question space.

***

### 3.21 The Technical Expert

#### 3.21.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium-low for general use; better as an answer persona or a topic-gated question generator.


#### 3.21.2 Evidence

- Low volume with narrow clusters around architecture, dependencies, and technical risk; reinforces existing implementation paradigms.


#### 3.21.3 Recommendations for Use

- Add when user prompts highlight specific technical unknowns (“how could this be built?”) and when risk of speculative architecture is high.


#### 3.21.4 Notes

- Reflects the research warning that expert-driven question generation can entrench current paradigms; should not anchor the overall question territory.

***

### 3.22 The Simplifier

#### 3.22.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium-low; strongly convergent via subtraction, better suited to Phase 3 and 4.


#### 3.22.2 Evidence

- Low-volume persona with consistent 4–5 cluster outputs focused on removal and essentialization.
- Rarely generates pure generative what-if questions; most questions are “what if we did not…?” evaluations.


#### 3.22.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use only when simplification is explicitly part of the user’s stated goal (e.g., de-scoping a bloated system).


#### 3.22.4 Notes

- When used in Phase 2B, should be combined with strong generative personas to avoid a net subtractive bias in the question set.

***

### 3.23 The Connector

#### 3.23.1 Suitability for Phase 2B

- Medium; connective but less structurally distinctive than Analogist, and somewhat more implementation-oriented.


#### 3.23.2 Evidence

- Mid-volume with cluster themes around synergies and structural parallels between subproblems, but without the deep domain-shift structures of Analogist.


#### 3.23.3 Recommendations for Use

- Use when the topic spans multiple domains that must be reconciled (e.g., UX + sustainability + business model), and when Analogist is already present to push more radical transfers.


#### 3.23.4 Notes

- Likely adds most value in Phase 3 and 4 where connection-making across ideas, not just questions, is central; Phase 2B can often get similar benefits from Analogist + Systems Thinker + Synthesizer.

***

## 4. Natural Clustering Taxonomies (Cross-Persona)

Beyond the validated Phase 3 triads, the mapping suggests the following **question-mode clusters** for Phase 2B under isolated-subagent constraints:

- **Analytical Probing Cluster:** Questioner, Analyst, First Principles Thinker, Devil’s Advocate.
- **Generative Disruptors Cluster:** Visionary, Provocateur, Analogist, Appreciative Inquirer, Momentum Builder, Constraint Flipper.
- **Human-Centered/Narrative Cluster:** Storyteller, Audience Advocate, Empath.
- **Systems/Strategic Cluster:** Systems Thinker, Futurist, Politician, Pragmatist.
- **Specialized Risk/Economics Cluster:** Accountant, Lawyer, Technical Expert, Simplifier.
- **Meta/Integrative Cluster:** Synthesizer, Connector (when used for question-of-questions and cross-domain scaffolding).

Designing Phase 2B rosters that explicitly draw from at least three of these clusters—rather than overloading the analytical one—is the key lever for mitigating fixation bias and ensuring diverse, high-quality question sets in an isolated subagent architecture.
