## 1. Uninvestigated Personas — Priority Assessment

These personas exist in the 22-persona roster but received no focused attention in I01-I06.

### High Priority for Investigation

#### 1.1 The Systems Thinker — **INVESTIGATE (priority 1)**

**Why investigate:**
- External research rates it "High" for question generation (deep structural/systemic questioning)
- Phase 3 selection guide positions it in the External Perspectives Triad (validated cluster with Connector + Analogist)
- I05 found the Analogist generates strong cross-domain questions; Systems Thinker should complement with internal structural questions
- I06 found Systems dimension severely underrepresented (6.5% across Phase 2B set), with FPT as the only meaningful contributor (13.5%)
- Systems thinking is explicitly called out in external research as a critical lens for second-order effects and feedback loops

**Research questions:**
- Does the Systems Thinker generate genuinely systemic questions (interdependencies, feedback loops, emergence) or does it collapse into analytical decomposition like the Analyst?
- How distinct is it from the Analogist (external transfer) and Connector (structural parallels)?
- Does it fill the Systems dimension gap identified in I06?
- What is its selflessness profile — do systemic questions open territory for diverse Phase 3 personas or only for other Systems Thinkers?

**Comparison target:** Analogist (from I05) for External Perspectives Triad validation; FPT (from I01) for Systems dimension contribution.

**Hypothesis:** The Systems Thinker should generate LOW overlap with both the Analogist (different cognitive operation: internal dynamics vs. cross-domain transfer) and analytical personas (different question type: systemic interdependencies vs. decomposition). If confirmed, it's a strong Phase 2B candidate to fill the Systems gap.

#### 1.2 The Connector — **INVESTIGATE (priority 2)**

**Why investigate:**
- External research rates it "High" for question generation (bridges domains, exposes hidden relationships)
- Phase 3 selection guide positions it in the External Perspectives Triad
- The Connector's "structural parallels" cognitive mode sits between the Analogist's cross-domain transfer and the Systems Thinker's internal dynamics — testing it validates whether the triad is genuinely non-redundant or whether two of three overlap
- External research emphasizes "cross-context lenses" as a distinct question-generation capability

**Research questions:**
- How does the Connector's "structural parallels" differ from the Analogist's "cross-domain solutions" and the Systems Thinker's "feedback loops"?
- Is the External Perspectives Triad genuinely complementary in question generation (as it is in Phase 3 brainstorming)?
- Does the Connector generate selfless questions that activate diverse Phase 3 personas?

**Comparison target:** Analogist + Systems Thinker (if investigated) for triad validation.

**Hypothesis:** The Connector should occupy the middle ground — more concrete than the Systems Thinker (finds parallels in other domains rather than abstract system dynamics), less prescriptive than the Analogist (identifies parallels rather than importing specific solutions). If the three personas span this spectrum with LOW overlap, the External Perspectives Triad is validated for Phase 2B.

#### 1.3 The Futurist — **INVESTIGATE (priority 3, conditional)**

**Why investigate:**
- External research rates it "High" for question generation (horizon-scanning, "what might happen if…?", scenario planning)
- Phase 3 selection guide positions it in the Forward-Looking Pair with the Visionary (moderate ~19-20% convergence)
- I03 found the Visionary is a strong Phase 2B candidate with high selflessness; the question is whether the Futurist adds distinct value or simply duplicates the Visionary's aspirational framing from a trend-extrapolation angle
- Anti-pattern warning from Phase 3 guide: Futurist "decorates shared ideas with trend framing" at ~35% temporal lens rate on experiential topics

**Research questions:**
- Does the Futurist generate genuinely distinct question territory from the Visionary, or is the ~19-20% Phase 3 convergence replicated in Phase 2B?
- Is the Futurist's trend-grounded positioning meaningfully different from the Visionary's paradigm-shift framing for question generation purposes?
- Does the Futurist show topic sensitivity (better on strategic topics, worse on experiential ones)?

**Comparison target:** Visionary (from I03).

**Hypothesis:** MODERATE convergence with the Visionary. The Futurist asks "what trends lead to what futures?" while the Visionary asks "what could this become if we reimagined it entirely?" Different enough for complementary value, but only at high effort where both can be included. At medium effort, the Visionary's paradigm-shift framing is more valuable than the Futurist's trend extrapolation.

**Conditional:** Investigate only if the Phase 2B roster expands to 6+ personas where a second future-oriented persona could add value. Lower priority than Systems Thinker and Connector.

### Moderate Priority for Investigation

#### 1.4 The Momentum Builder — **CONDITIONAL (priority 4)**

**Why investigate:**
- External research rates it "Moderate-high" for question generation (progressive "what if" chains, action-oriented questions)
- Phase 3 selection guide positions it in the Progressive/Elaborative Pair with Simplifier (near-zero overlap, productive tension)
- The description ("good at turning ideas into action-oriented question chains") suggests it may generate implementation-focused questions similar to the Pragmatist, which would make it convergent/evaluative rather than generative

**Research questions:**
- Does the Momentum Builder generate genuinely generative "what if" questions or does it collapse into implementation feasibility (Pragmatist territory)?
- How does its "progressive elaboration" differ from the Visionary's "aspiration" or the Provocateur's "provocation"?
- Does it show high selflessness or does it ask questions primarily suited to its own answer domain?

**Comparison target:** Pragmatist (not in Phase 2B but represents implementation-focused questioning); possibly Visionary for "progressive what if" vs. "aspirational what if" distinction.

**Hypothesis:** MODERATE-HIGH convergence with the Pragmatist. The Momentum Builder's action-orientation likely makes it a better answer-generator than question-generator (consistent with external research's "moderate-high" rather than "very high" rating). Lower priority for Phase 2B than personas with "very high" question-generation fitness.

**Conditional:** Investigate only if there's interest in filling an implementation-oriented question slot in Phase 2B (unlikely given the preliminary results favor generative over convergent personas).

#### 1.5 The Simplifier — **LOW PRIORITY**

**Why investigate:**
- External research rates it "Moderate" for question generation (subtractive thinking, focusing, but "can prematurely narrow")
- Phase 3 selection guide positions it in the Progressive/Elaborative Pair with Momentum Builder
- The description ("strips complexity") suggests it's inherently convergent/narrowing, which is the opposite of what Phase 2B needs based on I01-I06 findings

**Research questions:**
- Does the Simplifier generate genuinely valuable subtractive questions ("What if we removed X?") or does it simply narrow the question space prematurely?
- How does its constraint-via-subtraction differ from the Constraint Flipper's constraint-as-opportunity reframing?

**Recommendation:** **DO NOT INVESTIGATE unless the Constraint Flipper investigation (I05) is deemed insufficient.** The Constraint Flipper already provides constraint-based questioning with a creative reframing operation. The Simplifier's "strips complexity" mode is more likely to be convergent than generative, making it a poor Phase 2B fit regardless of investigation outcome.

### Low Priority / Not Recommended for Investigation

#### 1.6 The Synthesizer — **DO NOT INVESTIGATE**

**Rationale:**
- External research rates it "High for synthesis; moderate for raw question invention"
- Phase 3 selection guide retires it from Phase 3 brainstorming ("integration capability wasted in isolated brainstorming")
- Its cognitive operation (clustering, coverage monitoring, meta-portfolio management) is inherently synthetic rather than generative
- If included in Phase 2B, it should function as a meta-facilitator (Blue Hat role) monitoring question portfolio coverage, not as a peer question generator

**Recommendation:** Do not test as a question generator. Instead, explore whether a Synthesizer-like meta-role could monitor the Phase 2B portfolio for gaps (e.g., "We've asked many analytical questions but no emotional ones") and trigger corrective persona activations. This would be an orchestration architecture question, not a persona investigation.

#### 1.7 The Accountant, Lawyer, Politician — **DO NOT INVESTIGATE**

**Rationale:**
- All three are Specialized Lenses (outer ring) in the Phase 3 selection guide with explicit topic-gating requirements
- Anti-pattern warnings: over-application on mismatched topics (Accountant ROI-analyzes $15 purchases; Lawyer cites HIPAA for birthday parties; Politician sees factional dynamics in algorithm choices)
- External research rates Accountant and Lawyer as "Moderate" (strong for narrow evaluative questions, weak for opening space), Politician as "Moderate-high" but only for stakeholder/governance questions
- I06 found the Phase 2B set already has sufficient Analytical and Tactical coverage; adding domain-specialist convergent personas would worsen the imbalance

**Recommendation:** Do not investigate for Phase 2B question generation. Their role is downstream evaluation and domain-specific brainstorming in Phase 3, topic-gated per the selection guide.

#### 1.8 The Technical Expert and Pragmatist — **DO NOT INVESTIGATE**

**Rationale:**
- External research rates both as "Moderate" (better at answering/evaluating than opening territory)
- Both are explicitly convergent/evaluative personas (CPS evaluation/implementation)
- The Pragmatist is core in Phase 3 (always included) but its value is in *answering* feasibility questions, not *generating* them
- I01-I06 findings consistently favor generative over convergent personas for Phase 2B

**Recommendation:** Do not investigate. Accept the external research assessment that these personas are answer-generators, not question-generators.

## 2. New Persona Candidates — Gap Filling

External research identified several gaps in the current persona roster. These suggest new persona archetypes to test.

### 2.1 The Appreciative Inquirer — **TEST (priority 1)**

**Gap identified:** External research synthesis (Section 4, Area 4) found "under-representation of Appreciative Inquiry and positive framing" as a convergent finding across all three analyses. Current personas provide only partial AI coverage (Empath touches AI Discovery/Dream, Storyteller touches narrative/appreciative modes, Audience Advocate uses HMW framing), but no persona is explicitly responsible for systematic AI 4-D sequencing (Discovery → Dream → Design → Destiny).

**Preliminary results connection:** I04 found the Empath's primary mode is "emotional excavation" (including negative emotions like shame, fear, burnout), not dedicated strengths-based inquiry. The Appreciative Inquiry gap remains unresolved (Open Question #2 in preliminary results).

**Persona design sketch:**

**Name:** The Appreciative Inquirer

**Core mandate:** Generate questions that elicit stories of success, identify existing strengths, envision desired futures built on those strengths, and explore how to sustain positive change.

**Question types:**
- Discovery: "Tell me about a time when [topic] worked exceptionally well — what made it possible?"
- Dream: "If [topic] were outstanding three years from now, what would we see, feel, and celebrate?"
- Design: "What structures, practices, or principles would reliably create that future?"
- Destiny: "What small step can we take this week to move toward that future?"

**Framework alignment:** AI 4-D cycle, Yellow Hat (optimistic), Red Hat (aspirational emotion), partial Design Thinking (future-pull HMW)

**Expected dimension profile:** Creative (aspirational framing), Human-centered (lived experience of success), Strategic (long-term desired state), minimal Analytical/Tactical

**Hypothesis:** The Appreciative Inquirer should show LOW convergence with all current Phase 2B personas (including the Visionary, which is aspirational but not strengths-based) and MODERATE convergence with the Empath (both emotionally focused but different valences — Empath excavates all emotions, AI focuses on positive/aspirational).

**Test method:** Generate questions for the same 3 test topics (space party, tool library, habit tracker) using an Appreciative Inquirer persona prompt. Compare dimension profiles and cluster themes against the Visionary (I03), Empath (I04), and Audience Advocate (I04).

**Decision criterion:** If the Appreciative Inquirer generates ≥30% Creative + Strategic questions with strengths-based framing that no other persona produces, it fills a validated gap. If it collapses into the Visionary's aspirational territory or the Empath's emotional territory, it's redundant.

### 2.2 The Meta-Facilitator (Blue Hat Role) — **DEFER TO ARCHITECTURE DESIGN**

**Gap identified:** External research synthesis (Section 6.1, Recommendation 4) suggests "formalize a Blue-Hat/meta persona for question portfolios" — a role responsible for monitoring coverage and asking "What have we not asked yet?"

**Preliminary results connection:** I06 found dimension concentration risks (Creative at 3.3%, Analytical at 32.7%) that a meta-facilitator could detect and correct mid-session. External research recommends "embed bias-aware meta-questions" (Section 5, Principle 4).

**Recommendation:** This is not a persona to test via targeted investigation. Instead, this is an **orchestration architecture question**: Should Phase 2B include a meta-review step where a Synthesizer-like agent audits the question portfolio for gaps and triggers corrective persona activations?

**Options to explore:**
1. **No meta-role:** Accept the dimension profile produced by the selected personas (current model)
2. **Post-hoc audit:** After Phase 2B completes, a Synthesizer reviews the question set and flags gaps for human review
3. **Mid-session correction:** After initial question generation, a meta-facilitator audits coverage and activates missing personas (e.g., "We have 40 analytical questions and 2 creative ones — activating Provocateur and Visionary")
4. **Human-in-the-loop:** The orchestrator presents dimension statistics to the user and offers to activate corrective personas

**Defer until:** After the Phase 2B roster is finalized (Step 1 of preliminary results). If end-to-end testing shows the diversified roster still produces imbalanced output, meta-facilitation becomes a higher priority intervention.

### 2.3 The Lateral Provocateur (distinct from current Provocateur?) — **DO NOT TEST**

**Gap identified:** External research emphasizes lateral thinking techniques (Random Entry, Po statements, provocation, movement) as structural countermeasures to LLM fixation bias.

**Assessment:** I03 found the current Provocateur already employs absurdist provocation, inversion, and constraint-breaking. Its "question laundering" mechanism (provocation → "real insight" suffix) ensures lateral questions remain actionable. The existing Provocateur persona likely covers this territory.

**Recommendation:** Do not design a separate Lateral Provocateur persona. Instead, if the current Provocateur is added to Phase 2B and underperforms on lateral disruption, revise its prompt to emphasize Random Entry and Po techniques more explicitly.

## 3. Phase 2B Persona Selection Guide — Readiness Assessment

The Phase 3 persona selection guide (`idea-symphony/references/persona-selection-guide_Phase2C.md`) provides a validated model: concentric rings by effort level, validated clusters, topic affinity matrix, anti-patterns, and selection algorithms.

### Current Readiness: ~60%

**What we have (from I01-I06):**

1. **Validated individual personas (11 assessed):**
   - Core analytical: Questioner (retain), Analyst (conditional), Audience Advocate (retain), Devil's Advocate (conditional), FPT (conditional)
   - Generative candidates: Provocateur (add), Visionary (add), Storyteller (strong candidate), Analogist (candidate), Constraint Flipper (low priority)
   - Human-centered: Empath (add)

2. **Dimension profiles:** I06 provides quantitative dimension distributions for the current Phase 2B set and qualitative assessments for candidate personas.

3. **Selflessness assessments:** I01, I03, I04, I05 independently evaluated which personas generate questions answerable by other Phase 3 personas (high Phase 2B value) vs. questions primarily suited to their own strengths (lower value).

4. **Topic sensitivity findings:** All investigations found persona cognitive modes are stable across topics. This simplifies selection — unlike Phase 3, Phase 2B personas don't need topic-specific gating (except for Specialized Lenses like Lawyer/Accountant).

5. **Convergent findings on Creative gap:** I02, I03, I05, I06 all identified Creative dimension as the primary deficit (3.3%). This frames the selection problem clearly.

**What we're missing (blocks guide completion):**

1. **Validated clusters for Phase 2B:** The Phase 3 guide's cluster model (Challenge Triad, Human-Centered Triad, etc.) is based on complementary output with near-zero redundancy. We have partial cluster data:
   - **Generative Pair:** Provocateur + Visionary validated (I03 — destructive vs. constructive creativity, LOW overlap)
   - **Human-Centered Pair:** Empath + Audience Advocate validated (I04 — emotional navigation vs. design thinking, LOW overlap)
   - **External Perspectives Triad:** Partially validated (Analogist from I05), missing Systems Thinker and Connector
   - **Analytical overlap:** Analyst + DA validated as complementary *within dimension* but redundant *at portfolio level* (I02, I06)

   **Gap:** We cannot define validated Phase 2B clusters until the External Perspectives Triad is tested (Systems Thinker + Connector) and we resolve which analytical personas (if any) to retain.

2. **Effort level mapping:** The Phase 3 guide maps effort to persona count (low = 0, medium = 4, high = 7). The preliminary results propose medium = 3 and high = 5 for Phase 2B, but this hasn't been tested end-to-end. We don't know if 3 personas produce sufficient question diversity or if synthesis quality degrades below a certain threshold.

3. **Topic affinity matrix:** Phase 3 has detailed affinity guidance (e.g., Technical/Product → Technical Expert, First Principles Thinker, Simplifier). Phase 2B may not need this — the investigations found topic-stable cognitive modes. But we should validate whether *all* topics benefit equally from generative personas or whether some topic types favor analytical coverage.

4. **Anti-patterns:** The Phase 3 guide documents over-application failures (Politician on space party logistics, Accountant ROI on $15 glow sticks). We have no Phase 2B anti-pattern data because the current set is compositionally homogeneous (all analytical). Once generative personas are added, we may discover anti-patterns (e.g., "Provocateur deflates whimsical topics by provoking in opposite direction" — noted in Phase 3 guide).

5. **Selection algorithm:** The Phase 3 guide provides step-by-step orchestrator instructions. We need equivalent logic for Phase 2B, which will be simpler (fewer persona options, no topic-gating for most personas, no cluster-completion logic if the roster is fixed at 3-5).
